## What it is

A Tamagotchi-style virtual pet (the pocket pets from the 90s). Your pet shows up in the statusline:
- **Evolves** based on your coding activity
- **Health** and **happiness** change over time
- **Builds an emotional connection** with your sessions

For users who want gamification with an emotional narrative.

## Summary table

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| aitmpl category | `statusline` |
| Installs | 76 |
| Mechanic | Pet with health/happiness |
| Evolves | Yes, based on activity |

## How to install it

```bash
npx claude-code-templates@latest --setting statusline/programmer-tamagotchi-statusline
```

For Claude:

```
I installed the statusline/programmer-tamagotchi-statusline setting.
Show me the current state of my tamagotchi.
```

## What you can do with this

### Emotional connection with sessions

If your pet is going to "die" when you don't code, it pushes you toward engagement.

### For users who grew up with Tamagotchis

A nostalgia trigger.

### Long-term metric proxy

Pet evolution gives you an indicator of how consistent you are with coding.

## When it's NOT worth it

<Callout type="warning" title="Before you install">

- **For serious work:** [Context Monitor](/boveda/setting-context-monitor) is the grown-up option.
- **If the pet metaphor distracts you:** avoid it.
- **If keeping it happy wastes your time:** ironic — the setting can be counterproductive.

</Callout>
