## What it is

**2,715 installs — one of the most widely adopted settings.** An env setting with quality-of-life enhancements for dev work:

- `USE_BUILTIN_RIPGREP=1` — uses built-in ripgrep (faster than grep)
- `CLAUDE_BASH_MAINTAIN_PROJECT_WORKING_DIR=1` — bash keeps the project working dir (you don't lose the path between commands)
- `CLAUDE_CODE_DISABLE_TERMINAL_TITLE=0` — the terminal title updates with session info

## What you can do with it

### Faster searches

`USE_BUILTIN_RIPGREP` makes searches 10x faster than traditional grep in large repos.

### Consistent working dir

`CLAUDE_BASH_MAINTAIN_PROJECT_WORKING_DIR` prevents an accidental `cd` in one command from breaking the following ones.

### Useful terminal title

Know which session you're in without having to look inside. Handy when you rotate between several projects.

### For long sessions

Better quality-of-life = less accumulated friction.

### Combo with [Bash Timeouts](/boveda/setting-bash-timeouts)

Dev Utils improves UX. Bash Timeouts adds safety. A natural combo.

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **If you don't have ripgrep installed:** the setting expects it to be present.
- **If your custom workflow breaks with a maintained working dir:** check compatibility.
- **If you don't want the terminal title update:** disable it explicitly with `CLAUDE_CODE_DISABLE_TERMINAL_TITLE=1`.

</Callout>
