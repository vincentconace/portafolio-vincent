## What it is

Detects changes in `.env*` files and propagates env vars between your local development and Vercel (production, staging, preview).

Hook events:
- `PostToolUse` — when you edit .env
- `SessionStart` — sync at startup to detect drift

Solves "works locally but not in prod" when env vars weren't set in Vercel.

## What you can do with it

### Catch "works on my machine"

You add `NEW_API_KEY` in `.env.local` and Claude implements a feature with it. The hook detects that it's missing in Vercel and propagates it.

### Drift audit

At the start of a session, the hook compares local vs Vercel and alerts you to any differences.

### Multi-env safety

If your local and Vercel staging should match but diverge, the hook flags it.

### Combo with [Vercel Auto Deploy](/boveda/hook-vercel-auto-deploy)

Env sync + auto deploy = complete pipeline: local changes get propagated and deployed together.

### Combo with [Env File Protection](/boveda/hook-env-file-protection)

Env Protection blocks accidental Writes to .env. Env Sync propagates legitimate changes to Vercel. Complementary layers.

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="danger" title="Before syncing prod env vars">

- **Direction matters:** syncing local → prod without review = catastrophic. Configure an explicit direction.
- **Sensitive secrets:** make sure the hook does NOT accidentally push local secrets to Vercel.
- **No Vercel:** doesn't apply.
- **For multi-team:** one dev's sync can overwrite another's changes. Coordinate.

</Callout>
