## The problem

Ever read something and thought **"an AI wrote this"**?

The same turns of phrase, the same sentences, the same little tone. Every AI **went to the same writing school** and got taught to write the same way.

**Humanízalo** is an open source skill that detects those specific patterns and **rewrites the text** so it sounds human. It detects **40+ patterns** across 5 categories.

Repo: `Hainrixz/humanizalo`. Open source.

It's an **alternative** or **complement** to the [Claude Copywriter / humanizer](/boveda/claude-copywriter) — more patterns (40 vs 24) and it self-evaluates up to 3 times.

---

## Before vs after

**Before (typical AI text)**:

> "In the dynamic landscape of digital marketing, it's crucial to delve into the strategies that drive meaningful results. Furthermore, navigating these challenges requires a holistic approach."

**After (humanized)**:

> "Digital marketing moves fast. If you want your strategy to work, put your energy into what moves the needle — not into doing a little bit of everything."

The difference: the humanized version **says something concrete**, has a voice, and calls for action.

## The 40+ patterns — 5 categories

### Category 1 — Inflated content (8 patterns)

- "Crucial moment"
- "Vital role"
- "Significant milestone"
- "Unprecedented transformation"
- Vague conclusions: "The future looks bright"

### Category 2 — AI vocabulary (6 patterns)

- "furthermore"
- "crucial"
- "delve into"
- "landscape"
- "navigate" (in a figurative sense)
- "unpack" (information)
- "game changer"

### Category 3 — Structure (11 patterns)

- **Binary contrasts**: "It's not X. It's Y."
- Excessive passive voice
- Sentences all the same length
- Lists with forced parallelism
- Rigid A → B → C → conclusion structure

### Category 4 — Formatting (5 patterns)

- **Em dashes (—)** everywhere ⭐ pattern #1
- Mechanical **bold** in the middle of every paragraph
- Decorative emojis in professional text
- "Label: content" style lists
- Overblown headings

### Category 5 — Communication (10 patterns)

- "I hope this helps!"
- "As we'll see in this section..."
- "Before we dive in, it's important to..."
- "It's worth noting"
- Overly friendly tone

### Fun fact

The **em dash (—)** is the #1 sign of AI text. If your text has 5+ em dashes in one paragraph, **you already know who wrote it**.

## How it works — the 3-pass flow

Unlike a simple humanizer (1 pass), Humanízalo runs up to **3 passes**:

### Pass 1 — Rewrite

Applies the detected patterns. Initial humanized version.

### Pass 2 — Self-evaluation

Claude **reads itself back** and asks: "Does this sound human?".

It uses a 6-dimension system:

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Direct** | Does it get to the point or ramble? |
| **Rhythm** | Do the sentences vary or sound like a robot? |
| **Confidence** | Does it respect the reader's intelligence? |
| **Authenticity** | Does it sound like a real person? |
| **Density** | Does every sentence add something? |
| **Soul** | Does it have personality and an opinion? |

Each dimension: 10 points. Minimum acceptable score: **42/60**.

### Pass 3 — Refinement (if pass 2 fails)

If the score is low, Claude **rewrites again**, focusing on the dimensions that failed.

It loops until it reaches 42+ or hits a max of 3 passes.

A text **with no AI patterns but no personality** still sounds off.

- Personal touches

The result is text that **sounds like someone wrote it**, not a neutral algorithm.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Humanizing all content without discrimination

For technical docs, legal contracts, transcripts — humanizing can **break accuracy**. Save it for marketing, blog, social, and email content.

### 2. Not reviewing the output

Humanízalo is excellent but **not infallible**. Read the output before publishing. Sometimes it over-corrects and removes nuances you wanted.

### 3. Humanizing text that is NOT from AI

If your text was already written by a human and you run it through the humanizer, it **can make it worse**. The skill assumes input with AI patterns.

### 4. Feeding it giant texts

If you paste 5000 words, the results are less consistent. **Process it in sections** of 500-1000 words.

### 5. Accepting the first pass

Sometimes Claude finishes quickly at 42 points but could reach 55. Ask it explicitly:

```
> Score 50+ please. If you don't get there, do another pass.
```
