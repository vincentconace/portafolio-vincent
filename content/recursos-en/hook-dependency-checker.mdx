## What it is

**1,729 installs — the most adopted hook in the aitmpl.com catalog.** It runs three analyses on your dependencies after every operation:

1. **Outdated packages** — which libs you have that are old
2. **Security vulnerabilities** — known CVEs in what you use
3. **License compatibility** — licenses incompatible with your project

Hook event: `PostToolUse`.

For shops with supply chain risk (which is all of them today), it's probably the most useful hook in the catalog. There's a reason it has twice as many installs as the second place.

## How to install it

```bash
npx claude-code-templates@latest --hook automation/dependency-checker
```

For Claude:

```
I installed the automation/dependency-checker hook. Run it on the
current project and give me the report: outdated deps, critical
vulnerabilities, and conflicting licenses.
```

## What you can do with this

### Automatic vulnerability catching

Every time Claude touches dependencies (installs, updates, etc.), the hook validates. If it introduces something with a known CVE, it warns you.

### Keep libs up to date

A constant list of outdated packages. Useful for periodic update sprints.

### License compliance

If your project is MIT and someone installs a GPL lib, the hook flags it. Crucial for commercial projects.

### Combo with [Codacy](/boveda/mcp-codacy)

Codacy does deeper quality/security analysis. This hook is the quick first filter.

### Combo with [Deployment Health Monitor](/boveda/hook-deployment-health-monitor)

Deploy health monitor + dependency checker = pre-deploy and post-deploy covered.

## When it's NOT worth it

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **Token / time cost:** the analysis adds up. For huge projects, it can be slow.
- **False positives on licenses:** some analyzers get confused by hybrid or multi-license setups. Validate the findings.
- **If your CI already does this:** Dependabot, Snyk, Trivy cover the same ground. The hook complements, it doesn't replace.

</Callout>
