## The most common question

> "I already use ChatGPT. Why switch to Claude?"

Short answer: Claude and ChatGPT are **cousins**, not enemies. Each has its own profile. For some things Claude wins, for others ChatGPT wins, and for many they're interchangeable.

What **does** set Claude apart:

1. **It has real access to your computer** (with Claude Code) and to your files (with Cowork)
2. **Reasoning in code** tends to be stronger
3. **Long context** — it can handle huge conversations and files without losing the thread
4. **It's more honest when it doesn't know** (it hallucinates less, in general)
5. **An open ecosystem of skills + MCPs** to extend it

What ChatGPT does better:

1. **Image generation** (built-in DALL-E) — Claude doesn't generate images
2. **Native voice / audio** is more mature
3. **Consumer plugins** are more established
4. **A slightly gentler learning curve** for non-technical users

**If you work with code, long docs, or automation**: Claude probably adds value. **If you use it for casual questions and generating images**: ChatGPT is enough.

## The 3 ways to use Claude

This is the first source of confusion. Claude isn't "one app" — it's 3 distinct surfaces:

| Form | Where it lives | What it's for |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Claude (chat)** | `claude.ai` or desktop app | Conversations, questions, brainstorms |
| **Claude Cowork** | Desktop app (not web) | Working with your files, calendar, email |
| **Claude Code** | Terminal / CLI | Serious coding, technical automation |

Most people start with chat. Then, depending on their needs, they add Cowork or Code.

More detail on when to use each in [Claude's anatomy](/boveda/anatomia-claude).

## The plans — which one is right for you

| Plan | Cost | Who it's for |
|------|-------|------------|
| **Free** | $0 | Trying it out. Limited on messages and models |
| **Pro** | $20/month | Daily individual use. Access to Opus, basic connectors |
| **Max 5×** | $100/month | Heavy use. 5× Pro's capacity |
| **Max 20×** | $200/month | Very heavy use or professional work |
| **Team** | $25/user/month (5+) | Small teams, centralized management |
| **Enterprise** | Custom quote | Large companies, SSO, compliance, SLAs |

**Recommendation**: start with **Free to try it for 1 week**. If you use it every day and want Opus + Cowork, move to **Pro**. Most people stay comfortably there.

## Your first day

<Step number={1} title="Create an account">

[claude.ai](https://claude.ai) → sign up. Email or Google.

</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Try the chat for 30 min">

Ask the questions you'd ask ChatGPT and look at the answers. Some good prompts to start with:

```
> Explain [a topic you know well] to me. I want to see how accurate it is.

> Rewrite this text in a more concise tone: [paste some text].

> I have this problem: [describe something from your work]. Suggest
  3 different approaches to solve it.
```

Calibrate: is it accurate? does it get close to what you want? does it hallucinate or tell you when it doesn't know?

</Step>

<Step number={3} title="If you liked it, download the desktop app">

[claude.ai/download](https://claude.ai/download). Mac or Windows.

The desktop app gives you access to:
- Cowork (if you moved to Pro)
- Connectors (Gmail, Calendar, Drive, etc.)
- Partial offline mode
- Quick model switching

</Step>

<Step number={4} title="(Optional) Try Claude Code" last>

Only if you work with code:

```bash
npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code
claude
```

More detail in the [full setup guide](/boveda/claude-code-setup-zero).

</Step>

## What confuses people most at first

### 1. "Which model do I pick?"

Claude has 3:

- **Haiku 4.5**: fast and cheap, for small questions
- **Sonnet 4.6**: the default, for almost everything
- **Opus 4.7**: the most powerful, for complex things

Detail on when to use each in [the 3 models](/boveda/claude-tres-modelos).

**If in doubt, use Sonnet** (the default). You'll adjust with experience.

### 2. "Why does it sometimes refuse to do something?"

Claude has **safety guidelines** — it refuses things like helping with malicious code, dangerous content, etc. If it refuses something that seems reasonable, **rephrase** it, explaining the context (defensive use, educational, etc.).

Sometimes it gets it wrong and refuses something perfectly fine. It's frustrating but it happens. Rephrasing usually solves it.

### 3. "Is my data private?"

Anthropic's current policy (check the up-to-date version):

- **They don't train on your conversations by default** (consumer)
- **You delete conversations** from the dashboard
- **For enterprise** there are stronger guarantees (BAAs, retention policies)

If your data is very sensitive: **read the terms** before pasting it. And consider Claude Code local with your own MCPs if you need zero external data.

### 4. "Why does it hallucinate sometimes?"

All LLMs hallucinate sometimes. Claude does it **less than others but still does**. Especially with:
- Specific numbers
- Quotes / references
- Recent events
- Niche trivia

**Rule**: for verifiable info, **check it** or ask Claude to flag what it's NOT sure about.

More on this in [three mistakes with AI](/boveda/tres-errores-con-ia).

## Typical adoption pattern

How usage evolves for someone just starting out:

### Week 1 — exploratory chat

You ask it a variety of questions and see how useful it is. You decide whether to pay for Pro.

### Weeks 2-4 — daily use

You bring it in as a tool. You start to notice what it's most useful for and what it isn't.

### Month 2 — genuinely productive

You learn to write better prompts. You start using advanced features (artifacts, connectors).

### Month 3 — you build a workflow

If you work with code, you add Claude Code. If you work with docs, you add Cowork. You connect Gmail/Calendar.

### Month 6 — a system

You have your own skills, configured MCPs, automatic hooks. **Claude goes from "a tool I use" to "the infrastructure of my work".**

Most people do NOT reach month 6. Those who do never go back to working the same way.

## When it's NOT worth it

Honesty: it's not for everyone.

❌ **If you never use it more than 1-2 times a week**: the paid plan isn't justified
❌ **If your work is very specific and the outputs don't apply**: research first
❌ **If you're not willing to iterate on your prompts**: the first versions are always mediocre
❌ **If you expect magic that "does it all on its own"**: that's not how it works, it requires participation

For anyone who does work with docs, code, or recurring decisions, **the ROI is real**.

## Typical first-month mistakes

### 1. Requests that are too open-ended

❌ "Help me with the code"
✅ "Explain what src/lib/db.ts does in 5 lines"

The more specific, the better.

### 2. Accepting answers without reading them

Claude proposes something, and you copy-paste it to production without looking. **Bad**. Always review.

### 3. Endless sessions

Leaving the session open for 4 hours across 15 different topics. The context gets contaminated. **Use `/clear` between different tasks**.

### 4. Not using the right model

Everything on Sonnet → you burn through your quota. Everything on Opus → you burn through more. Learn the 3 models.

### 5. Expecting immediate perfection

The first 10 prompts of your life with Claude are going to be mediocre. **Don't get frustrated** — you iterate and they improve.
