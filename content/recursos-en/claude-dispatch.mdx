## What Dispatch is

Dispatch is a feature inside **Cowork** (the new way of working with Claude). What makes it special: it creates **a single continuous conversation** between your phone and your desktop.

They're not separate chats. **It's the same chat on both devices**. You type from your phone on the subway, and Claude has access to your local files + connectors (Slack, Gmail, Drive) + any plugins you have installed on your computer.

### A simple analogy

You leave a note for your assistant at the office. You leave. When you come back, the work is done. **Dispatch is exactly that**.

| Regular chat | Dispatch |
|-------------|----------|
| Each conversation independent | One continuous conversation |
| No access to your computer | Full access to files + apps |
| Only responds with text | Runs real actions |
| Different on mobile and desktop | Synced across devices |

## Prerequisites

### 1. Claude desktop app

`claude.ai/download` — available for macOS and Windows x64. If you already have it, **update** to the latest version.

### 2. Claude mobile app

App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android). A recent version — Dispatch only shows up in newer versions.

### 3. Pro or Max plan

Dispatch is a **paid** feature. It doesn't work with the Free plan.

### 4. Internet + computer powered on

**The most critical part**: your computer must be **powered on with Claude Desktop open** while you want Dispatch to work.

If the computer goes to sleep or you close Claude Desktop, Dispatch **stops**. It's like the assistant at the office — if the office closes, they can't work.

## Setup — 8 steps

### 1. Update Claude Desktop

Check that you have the latest version.

### 2. Update the mobile app

Search "Claude" in your app store and update.

### 3. Open Cowork

In either app (mobile or desktop), find the **Cowork** section.

### 4. Go to Dispatch

In Cowork's left panel, click **"Dispatch"**.

### 5. "Get started"

The first time you'll see a "Get started" button. Click it.

### 6. Configure permissions

Claude asks you for **2 critical permissions**:

| Permission | What for |
|---------|----------|
| File access | Read/work with local files |
| Keep computer awake | Prevents it from sleeping while Claude works |

**Enable both**. Without them, Dispatch doesn't work.

### 7. Finish setup

Claude verifies that your phone ↔ computer are connected.

### 8. Done

You can now text from Dispatch on your phone and Claude runs it on your computer.

## Use cases

### 1. Process an Excel from the bar

```
From your phone:

> Open the Excel "ventas-marzo.xlsx" on my desktop.
  Extract the 10 largest accounts.
  Make me a summary with totals and top 3 products per account.
  Email it to contadora@empresa.com.
```

You leave the bar, and the email is already in your inbox.

### 2. Email + Slack brief from the subway

```
> Review my last 20 emails and Slack messages from the
  #proyectos channel.

  Make a briefing with:
  - 3 urgent things
  - 3 decisions I need to make
  - What can be ignored
```

### 3. Generate a presentation while you have lunch

```
> Take data from the "Q1-results.xlsx" report in Drive.
  Create a 10-slide presentation with key points for Monday's
  meeting.

  Visual stack: clean corporate.
  Save it in Drive as "Q1-presentation-final.pptx".
```

### 4. Organize Downloads in the background

```
> Organize my Downloads folder:
  - PDFs → /Documents/PDFs/
  - Images → /Pictures/imported/
  - Videos → /Movies/imported/
  - Delete duplicate files (same MD5 hash)
  - Send me a log of what you moved and what you deleted
```

### 5. Smart search

```
> Search my Documents folder for all PDFs that
  contain the word "contract".

  For each one: name, modification date, a 1-line summary
  of the content.

  Put it in /Desktop/resumen-contratos.txt
```

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Trusting that the computer stays on

If your Mac goes into deep sleep or your laptop runs out of battery, **Dispatch silently stops working**. You keep sending messages from your phone thinking it's running. **Confirm** the computer is on before long tasks.

Tip: on Mac, `caffeinate -d` keeps it awake. And plug it into power.

### 2. Destructive tasks without confirmation

"Delete all the old PDFs" from your phone = catastrophe if it misinterprets. Ask it for **confirmation before deleting**:

```
> BEFORE deleting anything, send me a list of the files you're
  going to delete so I can confirm.
```

### 3. Expecting notifications when it finishes

**Right now Dispatch does NOT notify you** when a task finishes. It's a research preview. You have to check manually.

Workaround: ask it to send an email/Slack when it's done:

```
> When you're done, email me to confirm.
```

### 4. Multi-thread

There's only **one active thread** in Dispatch. You can't have 3 parallel tasks. For multi-parallel, use [Agent View](/boveda/agent-view).

### 5. Tasks that need your input mid-flight

If Claude asks for confirmation halfway through and you're on the subway with no signal, **it just waits**. For autonomous tasks, be explicit:

```
> DON'T ask me anything in the middle. Make sensible decisions.
  If something is critical, pause and let me know by email — not Dispatch.
```

## When to use it

✅ **Good situations**:
- You're out and need something processed on your computer
- Mechanical tasks you want to delegate while you do something else
- An automatic email/Slack brief in the morning
- File organization in the background
- Reports built from local data

❌ **Bad situations**:
- Urgent tasks (the latency + possible computer idle)
- Creative / iterative work (you need many back-and-forths)
- Critical tasks without supervision
- Real multi-tasking (Dispatch is 1 thread)

## Security — critically important

Instructions from your phone **run real actions on your computer**:
- Read files
- Move files
- **Delete files**
- Access your emails
- Send messages on your behalf

Before enabling Dispatch, **understand what Claude has access to**:

### Default permissions

| Access | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| Files | Can read / modify / delete |
| Email | Can read and send on your behalf |
| Slack | Can read and post on your behalf |
| Drive | Can access your docs |

### Security best practices

1. **Start with a sandbox**: test with a test account or a specific folder
2. **Destructive actions**: always ask for explicit confirmation
3. **Critical tasks**: review the output before closing
4. **Revoke access**: if you stop using it, **disable the connectors**
