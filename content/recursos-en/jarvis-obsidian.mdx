## The difference from an "assistant"

Tony Stark didn't build JARVIS in a day. JARVIS wasn't a chatbot — it was a system that **knew Tony**: his priorities, his way of thinking, his preferences, his historical context. It anticipated.

The difference from a chatbot:

| Chatbot | JARVIS |
|---------|--------|
| Every conversation starts from scratch | Knows who you are, what you did yesterday |
| Answers what you ask | Anticipates what you probably need |
| Generic — talks like Claude | Writes in your voice |
| Lives in a separate tab | Lives where you store your info |

Building this for real takes **~30 days of disciplined use**, not "5 min and done". This guide is the base setup. The system starts surprising you after a month.

---

## The pieces

| Piece | What for |
|-------|----------|
| **Obsidian (local vault)** | Your knowledge base — markdown files on your disk |
| **Claude Code** | The engine that reads/writes the vault |
| **4 custom skills** | Capture, connect, brief, voice writer |
| **Daily 20-min ritual** | What makes the system actually learn your context |

Everything runs on your Claude Pro/Max subscription — **no extra API key**, no parallel clouds. Your vault lives on your disk.

## Vault architecture

The structure matters more than it seems. Bad architecture → Claude gets lost.

```
~/Documents/jarvis-vault/
├── 00-inbox/              ← raw capture, unclassified
├── 01-permanente/         ← stable, connected knowledge
│   ├── conceptos/
│   ├── proyectos/
│   ├── personas/
│   └── decisiones/
├── 02-diario/             ← 1 file per day YYYY-MM-DD.md
├── 03-output/             ← what the system produces for you
│   ├── briefs/
│   ├── drafts/
│   └── reportes/
├── 04-voz/                ← your corpus so Claude learns your voice
│   └── ejemplos/
└── CLAUDE.md              ← the system's manual
```

**Architecture rules**:

- `inbox/` is disposable — everything enters here first
- `permanente/` is what matters for future-you — the notes you re-read
- `diario/` is historical — what happened that day
- `output/` is what the system delivers to you — kept separate so it doesn't get confused with your notes
- `voz/` are examples of how you write — Claude learns from there

## The system's `CLAUDE.md`

The most critical part. This is what defines "JARVIS" — its behavior, its priorities, its way of operating.

Minimal structure:

```markdown
# JARVIS Vault

## Who I am
[2-3 lines: your role, your industry, what career stage you're in]

## What I'm currently working on
[Active projects, main focus of the quarter]

## My writing voice
- Tone: [direct / warm / sarcastic / formal / etc.]
- Voseo / tuteo: [Argentine voseo]
- Words I DO use: [...]
- Words I DON'T use: [...]
- Structure: [start with the punch line / build up to context / etc.]
- References: [Maciej Cegłowski style / Paul Graham style / etc.]

## How I operate
- Important decisions → docs in permanente/decisiones/ with a date
- New learnings → permanente/conceptos/
- People who show up often → permanente/personas/`[name].md`
- Meetings / interactions → diario/`[date].md`

## Expected system behavior
- When capturing new info, identify whether it connects to existing notes
- Before "saving" a duplicate, SEARCH first to see if a related one exists
- If you find a contradiction with a previous note, tell me (don't silently overwrite it)
- In outputs (briefs, drafts), USE my voice (check 04-voz/ejemplos/)
- After each session, suggest 1-2 non-obvious cross-connections
```

You edit this throughout the first month as you discover what's missing.

## The 4 skills

### Skill 1 — capture

So that any "note this down for me" gets stored well:

```markdown
---
name: capture
description: Captures something into the vault. Activate with "note this down", "save",
  "log this", "capture", "for future me".
---

# Capture

When the user asks to capture something:

1. Think about what it is: a concept? a decision? a person? a project?
2. Search whether a related note already exists in `01-permanente/`
3. If it exists → **update** it (don't duplicate)
4. If it doesn't exist → create it in the correct subfolder with `[[wikilinks]]`
   to related notes
5. Show me a summary: file, section, changes

If you're not sure where it goes, put it in `00-inbox/` and let me know to
classify it later.

DON'T use tags. Use wikilinks.
DON'T mix captures with produced docs. These go in permanente, the
produced docs go in output.
```

### Skill 2 — connect

So the knowledge graph keeps growing:

```markdown
---
name: connect
description: Finds cross-connections in the vault. Activate when
  we ask about a topic or when a session ends.
---

# Connect

When a topic is mentioned:

1. Search all notes in the vault that mention the topic (grep)
2. Identify the 3-5 most relevant ones
3. Give me back the interesting (non-obvious) connections
4. If any connection surprises you, flag it explicitly

When a session ends:

1. Re-read the notes modified during the session
2. Identify 1-2 non-obvious connections between them
3. Suggest whether I want to make new wikilinks
```

### Skill 3 — brief

So every morning you start with context:

```markdown
---
name: brief
description: Generates the day's brief. Activate every morning or when
  I ask for "brief", "what's up today", "prep my day".
---

# Brief

Produce a brief in `03-output/briefs/`[date].md`` with:

## Focus of the day
[Look at active projects + what was left open yesterday in diario/]

## What matters
[Top 3 things that could move the needle today]

## Pending
[What wasn't closed in the last 3 days]

## People waiting on something from me
[Notes in personas/ with "pendiente_mio: true" or equivalent]

## Suggestion
[1 thing I noticed is worth doing but didn't tell you]
```

### Skill 4 — voice-writer

The subtlest one — writing in your voice:

```markdown
---
name: voice-writer
description: Generates content in my voice. Activate when I ask for a draft,
  rewrite this, write me an email, post, thread, etc.
---

# Voice Writer

Before generating:
1. READ 3 random examples from `04-voz/ejemplos/`
2. Identify my tone, rhythm, structure, favorite words

When you write:
- Use MY structure (not Claude's default)
- Keep the words that I use
- Avoid: corporate jargon, "It's important to note", "In conclusion",
  emojis unless they're part of my voice, empty lists
- If you're going to use a metaphor, check whether I use metaphors — if not,
  don't add one

After generating:
- Ask yourself: "does this sound like [my name]?"
- If not, rewrite it before showing me
```

## The daily 20-minute ritual

Without this, the system **doesn't learn**. Skills without a ritual are inert.

### Morning (10 min)

```
[You open Claude Code in the vault]
> /brief
[You read the brief it generated, decide your real focus]

> Capture what I decided: today the focus is X because Y.
```

Claude generates the brief, you read/adjust it, and what you decided to do gets logged.

### During the day (continuous capture)

When something is worth saving:

```
> capture: the client Acme told me that problem X they had
  with Stripe, they solved it by migrating to Y. Reason: latency.
```

Claude saves it in `personas/acme.md` or wherever it belongs, with wikilinks.

### Evening / close (10 min)

```
> Close out the day. Summarize for me what happened today, what got done, what
  was left open. Note it in diario/.

> Find cross-connections between the notes I modified today and
  the rest of the vault.
```

Claude generates the day's close + suggests connections. **The connections are the gold** — relationships you wouldn't have noticed.

## How it grows after day 30

The interesting part isn't day 1. It's day 30.

At 30 days your vault has:
- ~30 daily notes with real context
- ~50-100 accumulated permanent notes
- ~20-40 people with their history
- ~10+ documented decisions

At that point, **Claude starts being genuinely useful**:

```
> I need to decide whether we hire this new vendor.

Claude:
  Before deciding, look at:
  - decisiones/2026-03-12-vendor-anterior.md (criteria you used before)
  - personas/juan-vendor-actual.md (your relationship with the current vendor,
    includes that he promised things and didn't deliver)
  - permanente/conceptos/criterios-evaluacion-vendors.md (framework you
    put together in February)

  Based on that, I suggest these 3 hard criteria and 2 soft ones to
  compare the new vendor.
```

That doesn't come from a chatbot. It comes from a system that **knows your context**.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Expecting magic on day 1

Without 30 days of capture, the system is just basic Claude+Obsidian. **The investment is time**, not setup.

### 2. Capturing everything without curating

If you capture 50 things a day without thinking, the vault turns into garbage. Capture **what matters for future-you**, not everything.

### 3. Not updating the skills

As you discover how you want to operate, **you edit the skills**. The SKILL.md isn't forever — it's for this version of you.

### 4. Trusting the voice 100% too early

`voice-writer` needs 20-30 good examples in `04-voz/` to work well. Before that, it'll sound partly like Claude.

### 5. No ritual

Skills + Obsidian without a daily ritual = a dead system. **The ritual is what keeps it alive**.

## When it's not worth it

- If you don't have the discipline for 20 min/day sustained over a month
- If you don't write anything in general (there's no corpus to learn your voice from)
- If your work doesn't require remembering historical context
- If you think it's "automation" — it's a **living system**, it requires participation

For anyone who does fit the profile, it's one of the highest-ROI investments in personal AI.
