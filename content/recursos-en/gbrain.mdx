## The problem

You explain something to Claude. Tomorrow it's **forgotten**. You explain it again. Over and over.

Your client notes, your team, your meetings, your projects, your ideas — all of it **lives scattered** across your head, Notion, Drive, emails.

**Gbrain gives Claude a brain that NEVER gets wiped**.

Repo: `garrytan/gbrain`. 4k+ stars. Open source. Runs **locally**.

---

## What it is

Picture a folder on your computer with notes about your clients, team, meetings, projects. Or an empty folder. **It doesn't matter**.

Gbrain turns that folder into **Claude's brain**. Before answering you, Claude **checks its brain** to give you the best response.

### Example

```
> Prep me for the meeting with attorney Joaquín
```

Claude **already knows**:
- Who Joaquín is
- What you discussed in the last meeting
- What's still pending
- Project context

Without you having said anything in this conversation.

---

## How it works — the cycle

```
1. A signal arrives (email, meeting, tweet, link)
2. Claude detects entities (people, companies, projects)
3. Reads its brain (looks up what it already knows)
4. Responds with context
5. Updates its brain
6. Organizes and connects
```

### The wild part

**While you sleep**, Claude organizes its brain on its own and connects projects/people/ideas. **Smarter every day**.

## Brain structure

Gbrain organizes by **entities**:

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| People | "Joaquín García (attorney)", "Ana López (CEO of X)" |
| Companies | "Acme Corp", "My client B" |
| Projects | "Q3 launch", "Migration to Stripe" |
| Concepts | "Pricing strategy", "Brand voice" |
| Events | "Meeting 2026-03-15", "DXC Conference" |

Each entity has:
- Summary
- Connections to other entities
- Chronological history
- Tags

## Use cases

### 1. Personal CRM

```
> I talked to Client X. They asked for 3 changes to the proposal.
> Next step: send them draft v2 on Friday.
```

Gbrain saves:
- Client X (with prior context)
- Requested changes (linked to the proposal)
- Action item with a date

Next time you mention Client X: Claude **pulls everything up**.

### 2. Meeting brief before a meeting

```
> I have a meeting in 10 min with Client B.
```

Claude:
- Last meeting with Client B (when, about what)
- Project status
- Pending actions on both sides
- Personal context (client preferences, avoid mentioning X)

### 3. Project tracking without an external tool

```
> How's project X going?
```

Claude:
- Current status based on all prior conversations
- Open blockers
- Upcoming milestones
- People involved

### 4. Daily reflection

```
> Note today's takeaway: I discovered that my B2B audience
  responds better to use cases than to features.
```

Gbrain saves it. In 3 months when you use this insight, **it's there**.

### 5. Connecting scattered ideas

```
> Is there a pattern among the last 5 clients who churned?
```

Claude searches its brain, detects patterns, and answers with analysis based on data **you weren't going to connect manually**.

## Why it runs locally

### Privacy

Your brain holds sensitive info (clients, finances, strategic decisions). You **don't want** it living on a third party's server.

### Speed

Queries against local files are fast. No network latency.

### Sovereignty

If Anthropic changes its policy tomorrow, your brain **is still yours**.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Not feeding the brain

If you only query but don't feed it, **the brain stays empty**. Every time something important happens, **tell it**.

### 2. Duplicate entities

"Client X" vs "Client X Inc." vs "X" — Gbrain may not detect them as the same. **Review periodically** to make sure entities are consolidated.

### 3. Sensitive info without control

Gbrain runs locally, but **your disk can be shared, synced, hacked**. Treat your folder as **sensitive data**.

### 4. Expecting Gbrain to replace everything

Gbrain is operational memory. For:
- A real CRM with automation → HubSpot
- Formal project management → Linear
- A public knowledge base → Notion

Gbrain is a **complement**.

### 5. No backups

If your disk breaks, you lose months of brain. **Regular backups** are critical.

## When it's NOT worth it

| Case | Why |
|------|---------|
| Very operational work with no recurring entities | Without patterns, Gbrain adds little |
| Strict compliance that prohibits unencrypted local data | Verify first |
| Teams where info must be shared | Notion wins on collaboration |
| No discipline to feed the brain | Empty = useless |
