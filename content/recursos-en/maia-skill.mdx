## What it is

Picture **5 researchers working at the same time**, each specialized in a different type of market. You tell them *"analyze the market"* and they go off to research **in parallel**:

- One looks into crypto
- Another into stocks
- Another into currencies
- Another into commodities
- And the last one **combines everything** into a strategy

That's Maia Skill. It turns your terminal into a **market research center** with a team of AI agents.

Repo: `Hainrixz/maia-skill`. Open source.

---

## The 5 agents

| Agent | Covers |
|--------|-------|
| **Crypto** | Bitcoin, Ethereum, top alts |
| **Stocks** | S&P 500, NASDAQ, major indices |
| **Currencies** | USD/MXN, DXY, major pairs |
| **Materials** | Gold, oil, key commodities |
| **Strategy** | Combines the previous 4 and makes recommendations |

---

## How it works

### Real multi-agent

All 5 work **in parallel**, not sequentially. Your report is ready in **the time of the slowest one**, not the sum of all of them.

### Live research

They pull **fresh data from the internet** every time. Nothing is stored from before.

### Interactive dashboard

The report opens as a **web page on your machine** with:
- Charts
- Rankings
- Recommendations (with disclaimers)

## How to use it

Inside Claude Code:

```
/maia
```

Claude deploys the 5 agents, they research in parallel (~3-8 min), and generate the dashboard.

### Variants

```
> /maia focus on crypto and commodities
```

```
> /maia with a 3-month horizon, conservative profile
```

```
> /maia compare vs my current portfolio: [paste your portfolio]
```

## The dashboard

It opens in your browser. Typical structure:

### Section 1 — Executive summary

- Overall state of the markets (1 sentence per category)
- Top 3 opportunities right now
- Top 3 risks right now

### Section 2 — By market

For each category (crypto, stocks, etc.):
- Current state with key data
- Trends over the last few weeks
- Sentiment
- Upcoming catalysts

### Section 3 — Cross-market analysis

The Strategy agent combines everything:
- Detected correlations
- Hedging opportunities
- Suggested asset allocation (with disclaimers)

### Section 4 — Sources

Every data point with a link to its source. **Verifiable**. Not made up.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Taking the "recommendations" as truth

Maia throws out suggestions **based on public data + standard criteria**. It has NO insider info, does NOT know your personal financial situation, and is NOT a certified CFA.

**Always validate with a human**.

### 2. Trading on every report

If you blindly follow the suggestions = **you're going to lose money**. Markets are more complex than any automated analysis.

### 3. No source diversification

Maia + Bloomberg + your advisor + your own analysis = a robust stack.
Maia alone = dangerous.

### 4. Forgetting the disclaimer

The dashboard has disclaimers for a reason. **Read them**. Every decision with your money is yours.

### 5. Expecting it to predict the future

Maia analyzes the **present**. It doesn't predict. If any output sounds like "this is going to happen", it's **probability, not certainty**.

---

## Limitations

### Real-time data ≠ perfect data

Sometimes public APIs are delayed (15 min in many cases). For day-trading, that's not enough. For macro analysis, it's fine.

### No personal context

It doesn't know your age, situation, real risk tolerance, or specific horizon. Ask it to assume a profile, but **you validate** that it applies.

### Source biases

Public sources have biases (Western financial press, mostly USD-centric). For emerging markets or niches, **complement it**.

### No backtesting

Maia doesn't tell you "this suggestion would have worked X% of the time in past scenarios". For that you need real backtesting tools.
