## What it is

Anthropic released a plug-in for SMBs that lives as a **toggle inside Claude Cowork**. When you turn it on, Cowork gains **15 skills + 15 workflows** covering 6 areas:

| Area | What it covers |
|------|--------------|
| Finance | Collections, margins, monthly close |
| Sales | Lead triage, follow-up |
| Marketing | Content strategy, editorial calendar |
| Operations | Purchasing, inventory, vendors |
| HR | Onboarding, documents, reviews |
| Customer support | Ticket triage, replies |

It's like having **15 specialized part-time employees** inside Cowork — without hiring anyone. Each skill works as a slash-command that runs with the right connectors.

---

## The named skills (the most useful ones)

Of the plug-in's 15 skills, these are the ones that deliver the most immediate value to a typical SMB:

### 1. Invoice hunter

Chases down overdue or soon-to-be-due invoices. Every Monday:

- Identifies invoices due this week
- For each one, drafts a collections email tailored to the client (firm with the late payers, gentle with the new ones, cordial with the regulars)
- Sends nothing — it leaves the drafts ready for you to review

Get paid without being the one who has to write the awkward email.

### 2. Margin analyzer

Connects to your accounting system and analyzes margins by:

- Product (which ones have real margin, which ones you're selling "at a loss without knowing it")
- Client (clients who look big but leave you very little)
- Channel (which sales channel is the most profitable)

Generates a monthly report with the 3 concrete, highest-impact actions.

### 3. Monthly close

The SMB equivalent of [Claude Finance Monthly Close](/boveda/claude-finanzas) but lighter:

- Checks that all of the month's transactions are categorized
- Generates a preliminary P&L and balance sheet
- Compares against the previous month
- Flags anomalies for you to review

### 4. Lead triager

When a new lead comes in (form, email, WhatsApp), the skill:

- Enriches it with public info (LinkedIn, website)
- Classifies it: hot / warm / cold according to criteria you define
- For the hot ones: drafts an immediate reply email
- For the warm ones: schedules a follow-up on your calendar
- For the cold ones: adds them to a nurturing sequence

### 5. Content strategist

Generates your editorial calendar:

- Based on your niche + your audience + what you've already published
- Pillar topics + variations by channel (LinkedIn, blog, newsletter)
- Briefs for each piece with hook, angle, CTA

### 6. Contract reviewer

You hand it a contract (PDF, DOCX) and it gives you back:

- A 1-page executive summary
- Clauses that need attention (auto-renewal, penalties, exclusivity)
- Diff vs your standard contract (if you have one loaded)
- Negotiation recommendations

**It doesn't replace a lawyer** — it gives you the review draft that a lawyer then validates.

### The other 9

The rest cover: new employee onboarding, vendor manager, support ticket responder, sales proposal generator, payment reconciler, inventory monitor, purchasing planner, investor report generator, paid campaign manager.

## The 7 native connectors

What sets the plug-in apart from "Claude answering with general knowledge" is that it **connects to your real systems**:

| Connector | What for |
|----------|----------|
| **QuickBooks** | Accounting data, invoices, P&L |
| **PayPal** | Transactions, payments, reconciliation |
| **HubSpot** | CRM, leads, sales pipeline |
| **Canva** | Visual content generation and editing |
| **DocuSign** | Signed contracts, signature status |
| **Google Workspace** | Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs |
| **Microsoft 365** | Outlook, Excel, Word, Teams |

The skills **use these connectors automatically**. You don't have to copy/paste data between systems.

> If your stack doesn't include any of these, the plug-in loses much of its value. Check the list before turning it on.

## How to turn it on

3 steps:

<Step number={1} title="Turn on the plug-in">

Two parallel paths:

- **Path A**: go to `claude.com/solutions/small-business` and enable it from there
- **Path B**: open Cowork → Plugin Marketplace → search "Small Business" → Enable

Both get you to the same place.

</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Connect the sources you're going to use">

Settings → Connectors. **Only turn on the ones you actually use**:

- If you invoice with QuickBooks → turn it on
- If your CRM is something else (not HubSpot) → don't turn on HubSpot
- If you don't use DocuSign → skip it

Each connector asks for OAuth. The credentials stay with your provider, not with Claude.

</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Create a Cowork Project with your business context" last>

The skills work much better when they know about your business. Create a Cowork Project with a `CLAUDE.md` that includes:

```markdown
# My Business

## What we do
[1 clear paragraph]

## Who our clients are
[profile + average ticket + typical sales cycle]

## Our stack
- Accounting: QuickBooks
- CRM: HubSpot
- Payments: PayPal + bank transfers
- Team: 4 people

## Conventions
- Language: Spanish
- Tone with clients: cordial but firm
- Main products: [list]
- Discontinued products: [list — so it doesn't mention them]

## Hard rules
- DO NOT send emails without my approval
- DO NOT update prices without my confirmation
- Show me drafts before any external communication
```

With that, all the skills operate with context about your specific business.

</Step>

## The first month — what to turn on and in what order

Don't turn everything on day 1. Suggested ramp-up:

### Week 1 — Foundations

- Connectors only: QuickBooks + email + calendar
- Skill: **Invoice hunter** (collections is immediate ROI)
- Skill: **Monthly close** (try it on a month you've already closed, compare against what you did by hand)

### Week 2 — Sales

- Add connector: HubSpot (or your CRM)
- Skill: **Lead triager**
- Skill: **Content strategist**

### Week 3 — Operations

- Skill: **Margin analyzer**
- Skill: **Contract reviewer**

### Week 4 — Expansion

- Turn on the rest only if you need them. **Resist the temptation to turn everything on "just in case".**

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Turning on all 15 from day 1

Each skill needs a bit of tuning for your business. Turning on 15 in parallel means none of them ends up well tuned. **One per week** is realistic.

### 2. Trusting outputs without reviewing

These skills produce 80% drafts. **You're the 20%**. If you let the invoice hunter send collections emails without reviewing them, you're going to burn client relationships.

### 3. No real connectors

If you turn on the plug-in but don't connect QuickBooks, HubSpot, etc., the skills operate without real data and will give you generic output. **Without connectors, the plug-in is 30% of the value.**

### 4. No business `CLAUDE.md`

Skills without context = generic answers. Your business doc is what makes the Lead triager classify your leads, not generic ones.

## When it's NOT worth it

### 1. Your stack doesn't include the native connectors

If your accounting is Xero, your CRM is Pipedrive, your payments are Stripe — the connectors don't apply. Some work with custom APIs but you lose a lot of the "out of the box" value.

### 2. You're solo (1 person)

If you handle everything alone, maybe [9 prompts for Cowork](/boveda/nueve-prompts-cowork) is a better approach. They're lightweight slash-commands that cover something similar without the overhead of the whole plug-in.

### 3. Large team (>20 people)

If you already have a team, you most likely need something more custom. [Claude Financial Services](/boveda/claude-finanzas) (if you're in the financial vertical) or your own specialized agents are a better fit.
