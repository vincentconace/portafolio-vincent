## What it is

Every Inc. is a media company that writes about applied AI. One of its best-known theses is "Compound Engineering" — the idea that each unit of engineering work should make the next one easier. Not just shipping the feature; shipping the feature **and** clearing the path for what comes next.

This plugin brings that philosophy down to concrete operations inside Claude Code. The main plugin (`compound-engineering` by Kieran Klaassen) ships 41 skills and 6 agents focused on building "skills that teach Claude to do the next similar tasks better." It's meta-tooling: you use Claude to improve how Claude works.

The second plugin (`coding-tutor` by Nityesh Agarwal) uses the same principle to teach you: personalized tutorials that use your real codebase as the example, with spaced repetition.

It works in Claude Code, Codex and more.

## What you can do with it

### Make each task lower the friction of the next

The compound pattern is: you build the feature, but you also capture the learnings in a new skill, update `CLAUDE.md` with what you discovered, leave a command that others can reuse. The next time something similar comes up, it's already half-solved.

### Turn repeated patterns into skills

`compound-engineering` helps you spot when you're doing something for the third time and package it as a skill so Claude does it on its own in the future. Compare it with [crea-claude-skills](/boveda/crea-claude-skills) — Compound Engineering is the ongoing philosophy, "Crea Claude Skills" is the one-off pattern.

### Tutorials that use your own codebase

`coding-tutor` doesn't show you generic examples. It takes your repo, identifies a concept you want to learn (for example "how hooks work in this project") and builds the tutorial using your real code. With spaced-repetition quizzes so it doesn't slip away.

### Improve the team's setup, not just your own

The skills you leave in `compound-engineering` are shareable. If you work in a team and everyone uses Claude Code, what one person learns becomes available to all.

### Philosophy applied, not just preached

Every writes a lot about this. The plugin is the "show, don't tell" version. If the idea resonates but you don't know how to apply it, the plugin gives you the rails.

## Real benefits

- **11k stars** — the philosophy has real traction, it's not marketing.
- **Skills shareable across projects** — the "compound" works cross-repo too.
- **`coding-tutor` is unique** — there's nothing equivalent that uses your real codebase.
- **Works cross-tool** (Claude Code, Codex) — you're not locked in.
- **Accessible official guide** ([every.to](https://every.to/guides/compound-engineering)) — the theory is well explained.

## When it's NOT worth it

<Callout type="warning" title="Before you install">

- **If your usage is one-shot:** Compound Engineering shines on continuous work on the same project. For isolated tasks it doesn't add as much.
- **If you don't have a culture of capturing learnings:** the plugin is about capturing and packaging. If your team isn't like that, the generated skills will go unused.
- **`coding-tutor` consumes tokens:** spaced repetition + quizzes over your codebase = lots of calls. Watch your quota.
- **It's not a shortcut:** capturing learnings takes extra work. The investment pays off after several sessions, not on the first one.

</Callout>
