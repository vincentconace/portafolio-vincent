## What it is

**1,840 installs — one of the most adopted settings.** The reason: a huge number of people don't want the `Co-Authored-By: Claude <noreply@anthropic.com>` that gets added automatically to every commit.

Setting key: `includeCoAuthoredBy: false`. Result: clean commits with no co-authorship line.

## What you can do with this

### Clean git history

If your team prefers commits without model metadata, this setting achieves that.

### Compliance / org policy

Some orgs require that commits not mention external tools. This setting enforces it.

### For public OSS repos

In OSS projects where author attribution matters, you may prefer to have only your name.

### If it bothers you visually

For some devs, the extra line clutters the commit. Aesthetics matter.

## When it's NOT a good idea

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **If your team values transparency about AI usage:** Co-Authored-By flags where Claude was used. Removing it hides that info.
- **For compliance that requires AI disclosure:** some contracts/regulations require flagging AI use in code. Removing it may go against that.
- **For auditing:** the line helps with tracking what was generated by AI.

</Callout>
