## The myth

A wave of people buying the same expensive Mac Mini "to get into AI". It's a symptom of a deeper mistake: **believing AI is a hardware problem**.

It isn't.

If you're going to add AI to your business, the most powerful tool **already runs in any browser**, on the laptop you have today. What really holds you back isn't the machine — it's that **AI doesn't know your business**. And it's not going to guess.

Before you spend a dime on equipment, spend **one hour** answering 5 questions almost nobody asks you.

---

## The 5 questions

| # | Question | What for |
|---|----------|----------|
| 1 | What produces the result? | You isolate the steps that make real money |
| 2 | Where is it written? | You turn tacit knowledge into a doc |
| 3 | What shouldn't exist anymore? | You audit what's excess (don't automate fat) |
| 4 | What's the simplest version? | You strip the process to its essential form |
| 5 | How do I add AI? | Claude hands you a 3-phase plan |

You do the first 4 with Claude acting as your consultant. For the 5th you paste everything back and it hands you the plan.

---

## Question 1 — What produces the result?

The steps that **actually produce** your sales, not the pretty theory.

You'll be surprised how many people don't even know how their own business works. Before automating anything, you need to know **what moves the needle**.

### Prompt

```
You are a direct, practical operations consultant. Help me
identify which steps of my business produce my results
(sales, customers, deliveries) and which are just habit or theory.

My business is: [describe in 2-3 sentences: what you sell, to whom,
how you make money].

Ask me questions ONE AT A TIME (max 2 together) and wait for my
answer before continuing.

Start from the end: ask me the exact moment a customer pays me,
and let's work backwards from there to the first contact.

Your goal: by the time we finish, we have a clear picture, in order,
of the real path that takes a customer from meeting me to paying me,
marking which steps produce the result and which are only there
because "that's how it's always been done".

Don't let me dress it up. If something adds nothing, tell me.
```

### What to expect

15-30 min of interview. Claude will ask you:
- "How do you receive the first contact?"
- "What happens between them asking the price and buying?"
- "What do you do / what does your team do?"

At the end, you'll have a clear picture that many **didn't have before** doing this exercise.

## Question 2 — Where is it written?

If AI can't see it, it can't help you. You need to turn the conversation from question 1 into a **structured document**.

### Prompt

Continue the same conversation from question 1 with:

```
Take everything we discussed about how my business works and
turn it into a clear, ordered process document.

Structure it like this:
1. Goal of the process in one line
2. Steps in numbered order
3. Who does each step
4. What tool is used / where it's done
5. Roughly how long each step takes

Write it in simple language, as if you were explaining it to
someone who starts tomorrow and knows nothing about the business.

If you're missing info for any step, DON'T make it up — mark it
with [MISSING: ...] so I can complete it.

Deliver it ready to save as a file called proceso.md.
```

### The output

You end up with `proceso.md`, which is the **raw material for everything else**. Without this, the following steps don't apply.

## Question 3 — What shouldn't exist anymore?

You take your steps and delete what's excess. **There's no point automating a step that shouldn't even exist**.

First you trim the fat, then you add AI.

### Prompt

```
Here's my written process:

[paste the full proceso.md]

Audit it like a consultant who gets paid to find fat.

For EACH step tell me:
1. Does this matter to the customer or only to me?
2. What would happen if I deleted it tomorrow?
3. Is it done for a good reason or just because it's always been done?

At the end, give me a short list of steps to eliminate, with one
sentence on why each one. Be direct, DON'T spare my feelings.

If a step is sacred and untouchable, tell me that too and explain
why.
```

### What's going to hurt

You'll see that you have:
- Steps you add "just in case"
- Triple confirmations nobody asks for
- Internal bureaucracy that adds nothing for the customer
- Manual work that generates no value

Some will be easy to cut. Others will hurt. **Cut them anyway**.

## Question 4 — What's the simplest version?

You remove pieces, merge steps, make crystal clear who does what, how, when and why.

The simplest version that gives the same results **is the one you'll use to add AI**.

### Prompt

```
With the steps that survived the audit, build the simplest
version of my process that still gives me the same results.

Rules:
- Merge steps where it makes sense
- Eliminate unnecessary handoffs and waits
- On each step make crystal clear who does it, what they do, when and why

Deliver it in 2 parts:
1. The final process, clean and numbered
2. A short summary of what changed vs the original version and what I gained

This version is the one I'll use for AI — write it so that
anyone (or any AI) can follow it without asking me anything.
```

## Question 5 — How do I add AI?

This is where **Claude hands you the plan**. Give it all the context:

### Master prompt

```
Act as a practical, honest AI strategist, NOT a guru
selling snake oil.

My business: [what you sell, to whom, how you make money]

Simple process: [paste the simple version from step 4]

My current tools: [e.g.: WhatsApp, Excel, Gmail, Instagram]

What eats most of my time: [describe the heaviest part]

Context: I DON'T know how to code. Low budget.

Analyze my process step by step and give me:

1. In which exact steps AI would save me the most time or money.
   Order them by impact vs effort (easiest and most profitable first).

2. For each one, what I can do TODAY with just Claude in the
   browser, without installing anything.

3. What I'd need to take further down the line to Claude Code or
   automation, and why.

4. What's NOT worth automating yet (so I don't waste time).

Close with a 3-phase plan:
- This week
- This month
- Later on

Tell me the first concrete step I should take TODAY.
NO theory — actionable steps.
```

### The result

A **prioritized list** worth 10x more than any "AI for business" course:

- What you solve today in the chat
- What you take to Claude Code later
- What's NOT worth touching yet

## Taking it to Claude Code

The chat is perfect for thinking. **Claude Code** can:
- Read your real files (proceso.md, Excel, templates)
- Create things that stay saved
- Repeat work for you

You don't need to be a programmer. You talk to it just like in the chat — except now it has hands.

### Setup (one time only)

```bash
npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code
```

Or use the desktop app / web version. See [setup zero](/boveda/claude-code-setup-zero).

### Your first prompt in Claude Code

```
> You're working in my business folder.
  Read the file proceso.md to understand how I operate.

  I want to build the first automation from my plan:
  [paste step #1 by impact vs effort]

  Before creating or touching files, explain to me in plain English
  what you're going to do and why, as if I did NOT know how to program
  (because I don't).

  Let's go step by step. After each step, tell me what we did
  and what's next.

  If you need something from me (key, data, decision), ask me
  instead of making it up.
```

## The agent that repeats the method

The most powerful part: instead of running the 5 questions by hand every time, **package them into a skill**. You install it once and call it by name next time.

### Prompt to create the skill

```
> Create a Claude Code skill called "diagnostico-negocio".

  Its job: walk a person through the 5 questions that
  prepare a business process before adding AI.

  In order:
  1. Ask the questions one by one, waiting for an answer
     (what produces the result, where it's written, what's excess,
     what's the simple version)
  2. Keep writing everything into proceso.md as we go
  3. Audit which steps are excess and propose which to eliminate
  4. Deliver the simplest version of the process
  5. Close with a 3-phase AI plan + first step for today

  Create it with a well-written SKILL.md file.

  When you're done, explain to me in plain English:
  - How to install it
  - How to use it again next time without repeating anything
```

### How you reuse it

```
> Use the diagnostico-negocio skill to analyze my
  after-sales process.
```

It walks you through the 5 questions again with the new context.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Skipping the questions and jumping straight to 5

If you paste "build me an AI plan" without going through 1-4, Claude hands you **a generic plan**. The value is in the context you build.

### 2. Buying hardware "just in case"

If you haven't answered the 5 questions yet, **you don't need a Mac Mini or anything**. Your current laptop + claude.ai is enough for this whole process.

### 3. Wanting to automate everything at once

The 3-phase plan is **on purpose**. If you try to add AI to 8 steps at once, **none of them work**. Start with 1, validate, then continue.

### 4. Not writing down the answers

If you answer the 5 questions "in your head" without writing, **it's useless**. AI needs text. The practice of **writing it down** also clarifies your thinking.

### 5. Over-analyzing and never executing

The whole process is **one hour**. If you spent 3 days "preparing the questions", you're already procrastinating. Do it raw, come out with a plan B, adjust later.
