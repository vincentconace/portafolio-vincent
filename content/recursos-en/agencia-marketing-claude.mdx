## The thesis

A marketing agency has **3 core jobs**:

1. **Audit** what's already running (campaigns, ads, landing pages)
2. **Diagnose** what's failing when results drop
3. **Create** new content / creatives

Each one can be sped up with an open-source tool. Combined, **they let you do in a morning what takes a traditional agency a week**.

> Important: this **does not replace judgment**. If you've never run ads, learn that first. If you already have the craft, the combo saves you hours of operational work + separate Midjourney/Sora/Higgsfield subscriptions.

## The combo

| Job | Tool | What it does |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Audit** | Claude Ads | Audits campaigns on Google, Meta, TikTok, YouTube + 7 platforms with 250+ checks per ad |
| **Diagnose** | TopRank | Connects to Search Console + Google Ads to understand why traffic dropped |
| **Create** | Open Generative AI | 200+ image/video models without paying for separate subscriptions |

All open-source, integrable with Claude Code.

## Tool 1 — Claude Ads (audit)

### What it audits

Feed it your paid media accounts. For each active ad, it runs 250+ checks:

#### Creative
- Hook quality (first 3 seconds)
- Ad ↔ landing coherence
- Variants present (if they're all the same, that's a problem)
- Accessible typography / contrast

#### Targeting
- Overlapping audiences (you're competing against your own campaigns)
- Audiences too small (the algorithm doesn't learn)
- Audiences too broad (high CAC)

#### Bidding
- CPA / CPM vs your industry benchmark
- Abnormal cost fluctuation
- Budget allocation across campaigns

#### Tracking
- Pixel working correctly
- Critical events firing
- Clean UTMs

#### Compliance
- Disclaimers present where required
- Platform-prohibited terms

### How to use it

```
> Audit my active Meta campaigns from the last month.

Give me a prioritized report:
🔴 Critical (fix this week)
🟡 Improvable (worth reviewing)
🟢 OK
```

For each finding: file / ad / line + a concrete action.

## Tool 2 — TopRank (diagnose)

### What it's for

Your organic traffic dropped 30% last month. **Why?**

The obvious options:
- Google changed something
- You lost keywords
- A competitor moved ahead
- A technical bug on your site

TopRank connects to:
- **Google Search Console**
- **Google Ads**
- **GA4**

And gives you a structured diagnosis:

### Typical output

```
TRAFFIC DROP DIAGNOSIS

Period analyzed: last 30 days vs previous 30
Total drop: -32% organic sessions

DETECTED CAUSES (ordered by impact):

1. LOSS OF POSITIONS ON KEY KEYWORDS (-18%)
   - "buy product X" fell from pos 3 → pos 8
   - "product X reviews" fell from pos 5 → pos 14
   - 8 more keywords dropped significantly

   Probable cause: competitor Y published a long-form article on March 5
   with the same structure. Now it ranks above you.

   Recommendation: update your piece with +500 words and a new data point
   they don't have.

2. TECHNICAL INDEXING PROBLEM (-9%)
   - 47 new URLs were not indexed
   - Possible cause: sitemap.xml was not updated

3. SEASONALITY (-5%)
   - This is normal in your industry for this month
   - No action required
```

**3 concrete actions instead of "it could be a lot of things, go investigate"**.

## Tool 3 — Open Generative AI (create)

### What it is

An **interface** that gives you access to 200+ generative models without having a subscription to each one. Instead of paying for Midjourney + Sora + Veo + Higgsfield + Kling separately, you use this interface and pay for actual usage.

Models included:
- **Image**: Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, Midjourney (via API), Flux, Nano Banana
- **Video**: Veo 3, SeeDance, Sora (when there's an API), Kling
- **Audio**: ElevenLabs, Suno
- **Others**: upscalers, background removers, face swap, etc.

### Why it's worth it

| Traditional stack | Open Generative AI |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Midjourney $30 + Sora $30 + ElevenLabs $30 + ... | A single subscription or pay-per-use |
| Each one with its own login, its own prompt format | A single conversational interface |
| Want to try a new model? New subscription | It's already in the catalog |

### How it's used

```
> Generate a photoreal image of a product on a soft gray background,
  camera at 45° from above, studio lighting, 4K.

  Try with: Nano Banana, Flux and SD3 — show me the 3 side by side.
  I'll pick which one to keep iterating on.
```

You compare output from 3 models in one request. **Impossible with separate subscriptions.**

## The full "agency" flow

How the 3 tools chain together in a real case:

### Monday — weekly audit

```
> /weekly-audit
```

Claude Ads runs the audit. It gives you a report. You identify 2 urgent problems:
- A Meta ad with rising CAC
- A drop in organic traffic

### Monday afternoon — diagnosis

```
> For the ad with rising CAC: dig deeper. Audience fatigue?
  Creative fatigue? A change in the competitor's bidding?

> For the organic drop: run TopRank and show me the causes.
```

Both give you a structured diagnosis.

### Tuesday — create new creatives

```
> For the ad with audience fatigue: I need 5 new creative
  variants. Use Open Generative AI:
  - Style: editorial photoreal
  - Hook: different from the current ones (the current ones are product shots,
    these I want people-centric)
  - Variations of the pain point we cover in the copy

  Generate the 5 with different prompts. Show me side by side.
```

### Wednesday — implement fixes

```
> For organic traffic:
  1. Update the piece that lost position using TopRank's
     guidance (add 500 words + a new data point)
  2. Re-publish the sitemap.xml for the 47 unindexed URLs

  For the new creatives:
  1. Upload the 5 to Meta Ads as variants in the same campaign
  2. Set up A/B testing 80/20 against the originals for 7 days
```

### Friday — verify

```
> Report for the week:
  - Initial audit vs now
  - Creative variants: which performed best over the 7 days
  - Organic traffic: did it improve after the fixes?
```

**A week** of agency work: 1 day of yours with the tools.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Expecting it to replace judgment

The 3 tools assist **decisions**, they don't make them. If you don't understand marketing, the tools won't teach it to you — they only speed up what you already know (or make you execute faster what you **don't** know, which can be worse).

### 2. Auditing without acting

Claude Ads gives you 50 findings. **You implement 5 critical ones**, the rest wait or get closed as "not applicable". If you only audit and don't act, you're wasting your time.

### 3. Generating 100 creatives without testing

Open Generative AI can generate volume. **You don't need 100 images — you need the 5 that perform**. Iterate with discipline, not with quantity.

### 4. Changing everything at once

If you audit and change 12 things at the same time, you don't know what moved the needle. **Isolated changes** to measure the real effect.

## When this combo wins

✅ You run your own marketing (in-house, not an agency)
✅ Enough volume to make the setup worth it (>$2k/month in paid media)
✅ You know how to interpret the outputs and make decisions
✅ You accept that it's a process, not a magic button

❌ No paid media budget → there's nothing to audit
❌ You need executive reports with certified compliance
❌ You expect an "automated agency" that decides strategy
