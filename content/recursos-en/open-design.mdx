## What it solves

If you use [Claude Design](/boveda/claude-design) heavily, you know 2 problems:

1. **It burns tokens fast** — every design iteration eats into your quota
2. **Sometimes it feels generic** — the default output sounds like "standard shadcn UI"

**Open Design** (from `nexu-io`) is the **local-first, open-source, Apache-2.0** alternative:

- Lives on YOUR machine (not in Anthropic's cloud)
- Automatically detects your agent (Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, Gemini, Qwen)
- Uses it as an **engine** without consuming "extra" tokens — just the ones you already spend with your agent
- **Connected to a local model** (Llama, Qwen), **zero cost per use**

## How it works

### The daemon

Open Design runs a **local daemon** (a background process) that:

1. **Automatically detects** which CLI you have active (Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, etc.)
2. **Inserts** its capabilities as available skills
3. **Uses the CLI as the LLM engine** — it doesn't make its own API calls
4. **Generates artifacts** in `~/open-design/output/`

Result: when you work with Claude Code, **Open Design is an "addon"** that extends your design capabilities with no extra costs.

### Local-first

All your data:
- Brand templates
- Generated outputs
- Your personal config

**Lives on your machine**. Nothing gets uploaded. Total privacy.

## The key question — does it win over official Claude Design?

| Aspect | Official Claude Design | Open Design |
|---------|------------------------|-------------|
| Cost per use | Tokens from your plan | **Zero** (if you use a local model) or "tokens from your plan" if you use Claude |
| Capabilities | 3-4 (landings, slides, clickable apps) | 19 |
| Pre-loaded brand templates | 0 | 71 |
| Customization | Limited | Total (open-source) |
| Privacy | Goes through Anthropic | Local |
| UX | Polished, integrated into claude.ai | More DIY |
| Support / Docs | Official | Community |
| Setup | Zero (already in your plan) | 15-30 min |

**Open Design wins on flexibility + cost**.
**Claude Design wins on polish + native integration**.

If you produce design work intensively, the Open Design setup is worth it. If it's occasional use, Claude Design is enough.

## The zero-cost trick with a local model

To **really** get zero cost, connect Open Design to a local model with [Ollama](/boveda/cinco-repos-ai-engineer):

```bash
ollama pull qwen3.5-coder
ollama pull llama3.3
```

In Open Design's config:

```json
{
  "engine": "ollama",
  "model": "qwen3.5-coder"
}
```

Now every design:
- **Doesn't consume Claude tokens**
- **Doesn't consume credits of any kind**
- Runs on your local GPU/CPU
- Latency a bit higher than Claude (5-15s instead of 2-3s)

**Trade-off**: quality slightly lower than Claude/GPT on complex designs. For 80% of operational cases, **good enough**.

## Cases where it shines

### 1. Agency producing lots of designs

If you produce 50+ pieces per week, the token cost with official Claude Design eats at you. Open Design + Ollama = **zero marginal cost**.

### 2. Freelance designer experimenting

Try 20 variants of a landing to present to a client, without burning through a subscription.

### 3. Education / learning

Design students who can't afford Claude Pro can use Open Design + a local model for free.

### 4. Compliance / privacy

A client whose designs can't pass through third parties (NDAs, government, military) → local Open Design complies.

### 5. Fast iteration with a fixed brand

If your brand is "Linear-style" and you're going to produce 100 pieces in that style, **you load the template once** and it always comes out consistent.

## The honest part — limitations

### 1. More complex setup

Claude Design is "open claude.ai/design and you're done". Open Design requires 15-30 min of setup.

### 2. Small community

If you hit a bug or have a question, **fewer answers** than with the official Anthropic product.

### 3. Local models have a quality ceiling

For designs where you really need "the best possible model", **Claude/GPT still win**. Ollama is 80-90% but not 100%.

### 4. Brand templates can be outdated

The 71 styles are a snapshot in time. If Linear redesigns its website, **the template can become outdated**. The community updates them but there's lag.

---

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Asking for "Linear style" for a product that is NOT Linear

If your product is a jewelry e-commerce store, **Linear style doesn't apply**. Linear is a UI very specific to technical B2B SaaS. Use the template that matches your market.

### 2. Wanting to replace Figma

Open Design generates artifacts. **It's not a professional design editor**. For iterative review with a team, Figma still wins.

### 3. Not customizing for your brand

Even if you use "Linear style", **your brand is yours**. Customize the palette, fonts, tone. Otherwise it comes out as a "Linear-clone" that looks like a random startup.

### 4. Local model without a decent GPU

On CPU alone, models with 20B+ parameters are **super slow** (1+ min per generation). You need a decent GPU (8GB+ VRAM) for smooth use.

## When it's worth it

✅ You produce design work frequently
✅ You want zero marginal cost (with a local model)
✅ Privacy / compliance that prohibits data in an external cloud
✅ Your hardware supports a decent local model

❌ Occasional use → official Claude Design is enough
❌ You don't have time for a 30-min setup
❌ You need the highest level of quality → Claude/GPT still win
