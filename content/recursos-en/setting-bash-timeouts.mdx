## What it is

Configures limits for Bash command execution:

- `BASH_DEFAULT_TIMEOUT_MS` = 120,000 (2 min) — default timeout per command
- `BASH_MAX_TIMEOUT_MS` = 600,000 (10 min) — absolute maximum
- `BASH_MAX_OUTPUT_LENGTH` = 100,000 — max characters of captured output

Prevents hung commands (stuck network, script with an infinite loop) from consuming time and tokens indefinitely.

## What you can do with it

### Anti-hang

If a command hangs (network timeout, buggy script), it won't wait indefinitely.

### Token cap

The output limit prevents a `find /` from dumping MBs into context and burning your quota.

### Tune it for your workflow

- Fast builds (< 1 min) — lower the default to 60s for faster feedback
- Slow builds (> 10 min) — raise the max so it doesn't get cut off halfway

### For autonomous sessions

Long `/goal` runs: the timeouts keep Claude from waiting on something that's never going to finish.

### Combo with [Performance Monitor Hook](/boveda/hook-performance-monitor)

Settings cap the timeout. The hook reports which command is eating it up. Complete diagnosis.

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="warning" title="Before changing the defaults">

- **For builds that genuinely take 30 min:** if you raise the max too high, you lose your safety net against hangs.
- **If your useful output is large:** some legitimate commands generate 200KB of output. Raise the limit carefully.
- **If your environment is very slow** (slow network, small machine): the defaults may be too strict.
- **A global setting affects everything:** if your workflow has very mixed commands (some fast, some slow), a single timeout won't fit perfectly.

</Callout>
