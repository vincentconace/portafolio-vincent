## What it is

A **Microsoft open source** voice AI system. Speech recognition (ASR) + synthesis (TTS).

What matters to us: **automatic transcription**. You feed it any audio or video — meeting, podcast, TikTok video, sales call — and it pulls out the full text with:

- **Speaker identification** (who said what)
- **Timestamps**
- **50+ languages** supported
- Runs on your machine, **free**

Repo: `microsoft/VibeVoice`. 38k+ stars.

| Metric | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Languages | 50+ |
| Audio per pass | 60 min |
| GitHub stars | 38k+ |
| Cost | Free (open source) |

## Limitations

### 60 min per pass

For long audio (>1h), you have to **split it**. Claude can automate that:

```
> Long 3h audio. Vibe Voice has a 60 min limit.

  1. Split into 3 chunks of 60 min
  2. Transcribe each one
  3. Concatenate respecting cumulative timestamps
  4. Identify whether any speakers appear across multiple chunks
```

### Quality varies by language

- **English**: excellent (99%+ accuracy in good conditions)
- **Spanish, French, German**: very good (95%+)
- **Languages with less data**: variable

### Poor-quality audio

If your audio has heavy noise / multiple simultaneous voices / very strong accents, the **transcription may have errors**. For critical audio, review it.

### GPU recommended

For 60 min of audio, **a CPU can take 30-60 min**. With a GPU, 5-15 min. If you don't have a GPU, patience.

---

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Processing audio without cleaning it

If your audio has heavy background noise, **clean it first** (Audacity, RX). Garbage in, garbage out.

### 2. Not verifying speaker identification

Sometimes Vibe Voice confuses similar speakers. **Review** the first transcription of each new recording.

### 3. Expecting perfect transcription of technical jargon

Vibe Voice was trained on general audio. For very specialized jargon (medical, legal, niche technical), **review the terms**.

### 4. Not using timestamps

Timestamps are **key** for going back to the original audio if you want to clip or validate. **Keep them** even if they look like noise.

### 5. Dumping everything into Claude unstructured

If you hand Claude 30 min of raw transcript without asking for specific structure, **it throws back scattered info**. Always ask for structured output.
