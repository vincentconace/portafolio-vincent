## What changed and what's confusing

Anthropic turned on Claude inside the Office suite. **Excel, PowerPoint, Word** are in GA. **Outlook** is still in public beta. That's the important part.

What most people confuse: **there are 2 distinct surfaces** that live in different places and solve different problems. If you don't tell them apart, you'll set up the wrong one and think "it doesn't work."

| Surface | Where it lives | What it's for |
|------------|------------|----------|
| **Office add-ins** | Sidebar inside Excel/Word/PPT/Outlook | Producing and transforming specific files |
| **Connectors in Claude.ai** | Settings → Connectors on claude.ai web | Researching and deciding with context from your Outlook/OneDrive/SharePoint |

Both require a paid Claude plan (Pro $20+/mo). There's no free tier for this.

---

## Surface 1 — Office add-ins

### What it is

Claude lives as a **sidebar inside** Excel, PowerPoint, Word, or Outlook. It reads the open cell/slide/document and applies real changes with **tracked changes** (in Word) or review (in Excel/PowerPoint).

It's not a separate chat. It's Claude operating on your file, without pulling you out of the app.

### When it's the right fit

When the work is to **produce or transform a specific file**:

- Build a financial model in Excel
- Fill out a Board template in PowerPoint
- Draft a memo in Word
- Triage your inbox in Outlook

### How it installs

From Microsoft AppSource. There are two listings:

1. **"Claude for Microsoft 365"** — the add-in for Excel, PowerPoint, Word
2. **"Claude for Outlook"** — a separate listing, in beta

After installing, inside the Office app:

1. Open the file
2. Go to the add-ins tab
3. Enable Claude → it prompts you to log in with your Claude account
4. The sidebar appears on the right

### What it does well in each app

**Excel**:
- Explains existing formulas
- Generates new formulas from natural language
- Fixes errors like `#REF!` or `#VALUE!` by looking at the context
- Builds complete financial models on request

**PowerPoint**:
- Respects your template, colors, and fonts
- Creates native slides (editable charts, not screenshots)
- Helps you reorganize a presentation's flow

**Word**:
- Edits with **tracked changes** — every change shows up to accept/reject
- Responds to colleagues' comments
- Reformats long documents

**Outlook (beta)**:
- Organizes the inbox into 3 buckets: "yours," "Claude's," "junk"
- Generates drafts in **your voice** (learned from the sent folder)
- **Never sends without you hitting send** — everything stays in draft

### What it does NOT do

- It doesn't send emails without your explicit approval
- It doesn't support Office 2016/2019 (it does support Microsoft 365 desktop and web)
- iPad/Android have reduced functionality

## Surface 2 — Connectors in Claude.ai

### What it is

In Claude's web chat (claude.ai), you go to **Settings → Connectors** and connect:

- Outlook (email + calendar)
- OneDrive
- SharePoint

With OAuth, nothing to install. After that, the chat can **read** your mail and your drives to answer with real context.

### When it's the right fit

When the work is to **research or decide with context from Microsoft**:

- "Summarize the last 30 days of emails from client X"
- "Find me every contract signed in 2025 on OneDrive"
- "What meetings do I have with the pricing team this week?"
- "Compare the Q1 board deck with the Q2 one"

It doesn't produce files — it **reads** the ones you already have.

### How it installs

1. Open claude.ai
2. Settings → Connectors
3. Click "Connect" on the connector you want
4. OAuth with your Microsoft account
5. Done, the chat can now query it

## The clear split

| You need to... | Use... |
|--------------|--------|
| Build/edit an Office file | Add-in (Surface 1) |
| Search info in your Outlook/Drive | Connector (Surface 2) |
| Have Claude reply to emails (drafts) | Outlook add-in |
| Have Claude read emails and summarize them | Connector (faster for this) |
| Work in Excel without leaving Excel | Add-in |
| Cross-reference info across files | Connector |

**The two are complementary.** The common setup is to have both configured and use whichever fits the task.

## 3 tested prompts

These are examples to get started with each surface.

### Prompt 1 — Excel model (Add-in)

You're in Excel with an empty file. You open the Claude sidebar and:

```
Build me a revenue projection model for a B2B SaaS:

- 12 months
- 3 scenarios: pessimistic, expected, optimistic
- Inputs: monthly price, CAC, monthly churn, initial MRR, growth rate
- Outputs per month: new customers, churn, net MRR, projected ARR
- Sensitivity table below: what happens if we raise price 10%, 20%, 30%?

Use colors: inputs in yellow, outputs in gray, scenarios in different shades of blue.
```

Claude builds the entire model — cells with real formulas, sensitivity table, colors applied.

### Prompt 2 — Cross research (Connector)

On claude.ai web, with Outlook and OneDrive connected:

```
I need to prep for a meeting with the client Acme on Friday.

Find:
1. All emails with the domain @acme.com from the last 90 days
2. Any document in OneDrive that mentions "Acme" or their project name
3. The meetings we had with them in the last 6 months (calendar)

Give me back:
- Summary of the relationship (status, tone, pain points)
- The last thing we promised and whether we delivered
- The 3 questions they're most likely to ask us
- 1 thing we should prepare before the meeting
```

Claude queries the 3 systems and returns an integrated brief.

### Prompt 3 — Inbox triage (Outlook add-in)

Inside Outlook, Claude sidebar:

```
Triage my inbox from the last 3 days. Classify each email into:

🔴 I respond (with a draft in my voice, 2-3 lines)
🟡 Delegate to [team X or person]
🟢 Just read / archive
⚫ Spam or irrelevant

Show me the reds first. For each one, a draft ready in my learned voice
but do NOT send anything until I hit send.
```

## For companies — deployment

If you want to roll this out to a team, the flow is:

1. **Microsoft 365 admin**: approve the add-in under **Integrated apps** in the Admin Center
2. **Define scopes**: especially for Outlook (reading mail, drafts) the permissions are sensitive
3. **Claude plan**: you need Claude Team or Enterprise for centralized management (not just individual Pro)
4. **Training**: a 30-min demo for the team separating the 2 surfaces (otherwise half of them will set it up wrong)

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Setting up both but always using the same one

If you have the add-in + connector but only use one, you miss half the value. The "produce vs. research" split is the key.

### 2. Expecting it to send emails on its own

The Outlook add-in **never sends without your confirmation**. If you were expecting an AI agent that answers inboxes automatically, this isn't it. It's a draft generator + organizer.

### 3. Office 2016/2019

Not supported. If your company still runs legacy Office, this combo doesn't work. Migrate first, then turn Claude on.

### 4. Free plan

There's no free tier for this. If you're trying Claude.ai for free, the add-in won't activate.
