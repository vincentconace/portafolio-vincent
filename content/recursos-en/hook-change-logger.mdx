## What it is

Every time Claude mutates files (Edit, MultiEdit, Write) or runs Bash, this hook appends a row to a CSV with: timestamp, tool used, file path, action, details. Hook event: `PostToolUse` over the 4 tools.

Super useful for:
- **Demos** — at the end of the session, you show the CSV with everything that was done.
- **Audits** — which files Claude touched, and in what order.
- **Replaying sessions** — someone wants to replicate your workflow, so you hand them the CSV.

## How to install

```bash
npx claude-code-templates@latest --hook automation/change-logger
```

For Claude:

```
I installed the automation/change-logger hook. Tell me where the CSV
lives and show me the first rows after a couple of edits.
```

## What you can do with it

### Demos without video

At the end of the session, you open the CSV and show the timeline. More useful than a long screen recording.

### Audit trail

For sensitive or compliance projects: the CSV gives you a history of what was modified and when.

### Productivity analysis

How many mutations per session? Which files do you touch most often? What was your time-to-first-edit?

### Compared to [Change Tracker](/boveda/hook-change-tracker)

Change Tracker is simpler (basic log, 1,127 installs). Change Logger is more complete (structured CSV). For serious demos, use this one. For casual tracking, the other.

## When it's NOT worth it

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **If you just want to know what Claude touched:** `git status` and `git diff` already give you that.
- **For very long sessions:** the CSV grows. Clean it up periodically.
- **Sensitive data:** the `details` can include snippets. If you handle secrets, check exactly what gets logged.

</Callout>
