## What Apify is (60 seconds)

Apify is a platform with **1500+ ready-made scrapers** ("actors", in their jargon). Each one pulls info from a specific site:

- Google Maps Actor → listings with phone, address, rating
- LinkedIn Companies → companies with size, industry, founded year
- Instagram Profile → posts, followers, public contact info
- Facebook Pages → reviews, contact, recent posts
- ... and 1500 more for specific sites

The difference versus writing your own scraper (e.g. [Scrapling](/boveda/scrapling-leads)):

| Apify | Your own scraper |
|-------|----------------|
| Already built, you just configure it | You build it from scratch |
| Maintenance included (if the site changes, they fix it) | You maintain it |
| Paid plan ($$$ per usage) | Just your machine/time |
| Output in standard formats | You format it yourself |

For people who don't want to code and need lists NOW, Apify wins. For heavy use or custom cases, doing it yourself is cheaper long term.

## MCP connection

Apify exposes an official MCP. Inside Claude:

### Setup

`.claude/settings.json`:

```json
{
  "mcpServers": {
    "apify": {
      "command": "npx",
      "args": ["-y", "@apify/mcp-server"],
      "env": {
        "APIFY_TOKEN": "${APIFY_TOKEN}"
      }
    }
  }
}
```

You get the token from your account at `apify.com` → Account → Integrations.

### Test it

```
> List the 5 most useful Apify actors for pulling B2B leads.
```

Claude queries the Apify catalog and returns the actors with a description and cost per run.

## Use cases

### Case 1 — Local B2B leads

```
> I need a list of gyms in downtown Buenos Aires.
  For each one I want:
  - Name
  - Address
  - Phone
  - Website URL
  - Google rating and number of reviews
  - Exact category (Crossfit / Functional / Traditional / etc.)

  Use Apify's Google Maps actor. Filter for those with a rating >4
  and more than 20 reviews (signals of a real operation).

  Output CSV to /tmp/gimnasios.csv
```

Claude invokes the actor, waits for the result (typically 1-3 min), and returns the CSV ready to go.

### Case 2 — Target account enrichment

You have a CSV with 50 companies but only the name:

```
> Take /tmp/empresas-input.csv and for each company look it up on LinkedIn
  Companies via Apify:
  - LinkedIn URL
  - Size (employee range)
  - Industry
  - Founding year
  - Headquarters

  If you don't find an exact match, flag "uncertain match" and pass the 2
  most likely results.

  Output enriched.csv
```

### Case 3 — Competitor research on social media

```
> My competitor X has an Instagram account @competidor_x.

  I want to see:
  - Last 30 posts with engagement (likes, comments)
  - Most-used hashtags
  - Posting frequency (how many per week, which days)
  - Their 5 posts with the MOST engagement (what they have in common)

  Use Apify's Instagram Profile Scraper.

  Return a structured analysis, not just raw data.
```

Useful for defining your own content strategy based on what works for the competition.

### Case 4 — Recurring monitoring

```
> Every Monday at 9am:

  1. Pull the new Google Maps reviews for my business (apify)
  2. Filter them against the ones I already have in reviews-archive.csv
  3. For the new ones, classify them: positive / negative / mixed
  4. For the negative ones, draft a reply
  5. Send me the summary on Slack
```

Combined with [hooks](/boveda/hooks-automatizacion) or with [n8n MCP](/boveda/plugin-n8n-mcp), it runs on its own every Monday.

## The real cost

Apify has a free plan with limited credits, then it scales by usage.

| Plan | Approx cost | For what |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Testing, occasional cases |
| Starter | $49/month | 1-2 lists per week |
| Scale | $499/month | Recurring pipelines |

Each actor has its own price per run (it varies: $0.01 to $5 depending on how heavy it is).

**Realistic math**: a list of 500 Google Maps leads costs ~$2-5. If it saves you 4 hours of scraping by hand, the ROI is clear.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Using Apify for everything

If each list costs $2-5 and you build 50 lists per month, that's $100-250/month. **High volume = consider Scrapling or your own**.

### 2. Trusting scraped data 100%

Apify is good but **it's not perfect**. Outdated addresses, old phone numbers, info that changed. **Validate before running outreach**.

### 3. Mass email harvesting

As with any scraping: pulling emails and doing mass cold spam ends with your domain blacklisted. **Outreach allowed, spam not.**

### 4. Skipping the reading of the scraped sites' Terms

LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook **prohibit scraping in their Terms**. Apify makes it technically possible, but the legal risk is yours. Sites like Google Maps are more permissive for reasonable commercial use.

---

## The legal warning (same as Scrapling)

Scraping is a **legal gray zone**:

- ✅ Public info without login, reasonable use, respecting robots.txt
- ⚠️ Large volumes, personal data, sites with restrictive Terms
- ❌ Paywall bypass, login with fake credentials, republishing content

Anti-spam laws (CAN-SPAM, LGPD, GDPR) **apply to the use**, not to the scraping itself. Collecting emails is 50% of the risk; sending them mass cold spam is the other 50%.

If you work in B2B with respectful outreach to sites with public info, it's generally fine. **If your plan is mass spam, no tool will save you.**

## When Apify wins

✅ You need lists from popular sites (Maps, LinkedIn, etc.)
✅ Low-to-medium use (no more than $100/month in credits)
✅ You want zero scraper maintenance
✅ You need clean, standard output

❌ Very high volume or zero budget → another solution
❌ Very custom sites not covered → your own MCP
❌ Strict compliance on your client's side → each platform's official API
