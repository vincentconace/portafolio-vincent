## What it is

The simple version of [Change Logger](/boveda/hook-change-logger): it records which files were modified and when. No structured CSV, no details — just the basic log.

Its 1,127 installs make it the most-adopted hook in the `development-tools` category. Why: it covers 80% of casual use without the extra complexity.

Hook event: `PostToolUse`.

## What you can do with it

### Basic tracking

Know which files Claude touched in the current session. Handy when you lose track.

### Combined with [Conventional Commits](/boveda/hook-conventional-commits)

If you commit every session, the log helps you put together a complete commit message by reminding you what changed.

### For autonomous sessions

You launch Claude with `/goal` and walk away. When you come back, the log tells you what it touched.

## When it's NOT worth it

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **If you want more structure:** use [Change Logger](/boveda/hook-change-logger) with CSV.
- **If git is already enough:** `git status` + `git log` have the same and more.
- **For serious audits:** this hook is informational, not compliance-grade.

</Callout>
