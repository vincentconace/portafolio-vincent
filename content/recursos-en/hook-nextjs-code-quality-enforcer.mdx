## What it is

**1,395 installs — #3 in the entire catalog.** The reason: Next.js is the most popular framework for AI-assisted frontend work, and enforcing its specific conventions is non-trivial.

After every edit, the hook validates:
- File structure (`app/` vs `pages/`, nested layouts, route groups)
- Component patterns (Server vs Client, naming conventions)
- TypeScript usage (proper types, no `any` without reason)
- General Next.js best practices

Hook event: `PostToolUse`.

## What you can do with it

### Keep Next.js conventions consistent

Claude tends to mix App Router and Pages Router when it isn't clear. The hook detects inconsistencies.

### Server vs Client Components

The most common bug in App Router: importing `'use client'` code from a Server Component. The hook catches it.

### Strict TypeScript

If your project is TS strict, the hook validates that Claude doesn't introduce `any` without reason or incomplete types.

### Combo with [Lint On Save](/boveda/hook-lint-on-save)

General lint + Next.js-specific = double coverage.

### Combo with [DeepGraph Next.js MCP](/boveda/mcp-deepgraph-nextjs)

The hook validates every edit. DeepGraph gives you a view of the whole project. Combined: micro + macro analysis.

## When it's NOT worth it

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **If your project isn't Next.js:** it adds nothing.
- **If your team has conventions that differ from the defaults:** adjust the rules or this hook generates false positives.
- **For small Next.js projects:** normal linting is enough.
- **If your team already has a custom linter for Next.js:** it duplicates.

</Callout>
