## What it is

**1,896 installs — #4 in the catalog.** The reason: it catches a subtle but costly problem.

When Claude uses the WebSearch tool and you ask "what's new in React" without specifying a year, the results might be from 2022 without you noticing. This hook intercepts the query and adds the current year (`2026` right now).

It goes from "what's new in React" → "what's new in React 2026". Fresh results guaranteed.

Hook event: `PreToolUse` (WebSearch).

## What you can do with this

### Anti-stale info in searches

Without the hook, "Next.js best practices" might bring you results from 2021. With the hook, 2026.

### For info that changes fast

Frameworks, tools, AI tools — anything fast-moving. The hook is basically mandatory for these searches.

### Combo with [Context7 MCP](/boveda/mcp-context7)

Context7 covers library docs (specific versions). This hook covers web searches. They cover different angles of the "Claude using old info" problem.

### Zero cost, high benefit

It doesn't consume tokens (it modifies the query beforehand) and adds no noticeable latency. The benefit: fresher searches.

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **For historical searches:** "first iPhone" doesn't need "first iPhone 2026".
- **For timeless info:** "what is OAuth" — the year adds nothing.
- **If your usage is 100% specific docs:** [Context7](/boveda/mcp-context7) covers it better.

</Callout>
