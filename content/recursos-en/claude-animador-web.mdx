## Why your designs look flat

You ask Claude for a landing page. It comes out **correct**. Nice-looking. But something doesn't click. Something feels **flat**.

The problem is the **lack of motion**. Without animation:
- Everything appears at once on load
- Buttons don't react to hover
- Scrolling is static
- Transitions are hard cuts

Result: **it looks like a PowerPoint presentation**, not a real app.

**Animation is the difference between a drawing and a movie**. It turns the "pretty but flat page" into something that feels alive.

## What gets animated

### 1. Buttons

Without animation: you click and nothing visible changes (except the action).

With animation: hover smooths color/shadow, click makes a visible press (scale 0.98), integrated loading spinner. **Immediate feedback** that confirms to the user that something is happening.

### 2. Reveals on scroll

Without animation: sections appear instantly on load.

With animation: each section does a **fade-in + slight slide-up** when it enters the viewport. Images with blur-to-sharp. Stagger between related items (e.g. the 3 feature columns appear with a 100ms delay between each one).

### 3. Page transitions

Without animation: you navigate from A to B, with a white "flash" between pages.

With animation: **smooth transition** that keeps visual context (the page fades out, the next one fades in). It feels like an app, not a static website.

### 4. Interactions

- **Menus**: open with a smooth radial or vertical animation (not an "instant pop")
- **Tooltips**: appear with scale 0.95 → 1 + fade
- **Cards on hover**: lift with shadow + subtle scale 1.02
- **Forms**: animated focus rings, errors that slide-in

### 5. Micro-interactions

- Heart icon that beats when you activate it
- Counter that counts upward instead of appearing at the final number
- Progress bars that fill smoothly
- Skeleton loaders instead of generic spinners

## The secret of timing

What separates amateur animation from professional **isn't the quantity** — it's the **timing**:

| Aspect | Amateur | Professional |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Hover duration | 50ms or 500ms | **150-200ms** |
| Curve | `linear` or default `ease` | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` |
| Stagger between items | no delay or 500ms | **50-100ms** |
| Page transitions | 1 second+ | **300-400ms** |
| Spring vs linear | always one | depends on the case |

Emil's skill puts these values in by default. It saves you **years of learning motion design**.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Animating everything

If **everything** moves, **nothing draws attention**. Animate what needs feedback (clicks, scroll), leave passive content still.

### 2. Long durations

500ms for hover feels slow and breaks the flow. **150-200ms** is the sweet spot. When in doubt, make it faster.

### 3. Loop animations in the hero

Constant loops (bouncing arrows, pulsing borders for no reason) **distract from the real CTA**. Use animations that **end** (one-shot when entering the viewport).

### 4. No respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`

Some users disable animations due to vestibular sensitivity. **Respect** the media query. The 3 skills handle it by default, but verify.

### 5. Ignored performance

Animating `width`/`height` causes reflow. Animate `transform` and `opacity`. If your animation lags on mobile, it doesn't add anything — it just annoys.

## When it's NOT a good fit

| Case | Why |
|------|-------|
| Very formal enterprise app | Excessive animations look unprofessional |
| Mobile-first with critical perf | Heavy animations kill performance |
| Older audience / high accessibility | Risk of vestibular discomfort |
| Deliberately boring technical product (CLI, terminal-like) | The product's charm is the "no design" |
