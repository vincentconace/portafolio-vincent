## What they are

Three prompts to organize your **month, your week and your day**, connected to your **Google Calendar**. Each one asks you the questions interactively (with buttons) and creates the events for you.

The clever part is how it separates what goes to the calendar from what doesn't:

<Callout type="info" title="Events vs. Tasks">
- **Events** (work, meetings, doctor's appointments, activities) → they're created in your **Google Calendar** (primary calendar).
- **Tasks** (to-dos with no fixed time) → they stay as a **checklist in the chat**, with a suggested time based on the day's free gaps. They don't take up space on the calendar.
- **Hybrid questions:** categories, priorities and confirmations use **buttons**; you only type the open-ended parts (specific times, your task list).
- Before creating any event, you always see a **summary** and wait for your **"OK"**.
</Callout>

## How to use them: from the biggest to the smallest

<Step number={1} title="The month — on the 1st">

Paste the **Monthly Prompt** to get the bird's-eye view: important dates, deadlines, due dates and your goals for the month.

</Step>

<Step number={2} title="The week — on Monday">

Paste the **Weekly Prompt** at the start of the week. You bring the month down to the week: you load the fixed events (work, meetings, activities) and the tasks stay as a checklist.

</Step>

<Step number={3} title="The day — every morning" last>

Paste the **Daily Prompt** to build your day: the schedule with times at the top and the tasks with a suggested time at the bottom, in the free gaps.

</Step>

<Callout type="tip" title="Automate it (optional)">
You can schedule **automatic reminders** so Claude messages you on its own: a **daily** one (every morning) and a **monthly** one (on the 1st). That way you don't have to remember to paste the prompt.
</Callout>

## Adjustments you can ask for

These prompts are a starting point. At any time you can ask Claude to:

- **Change the time of the** automatic **reminders**, or pause them.
- **Send the tasks to the calendar** as time blocks, instead of leaving them only in the chat.
- **Send the tasks to Notion** (if you have it connected) instead of the chat.
- **Use another calendar** (for example "Family") for certain types of events.
- **Add an end-of-day wrap-up**: in the afternoon it asks you what's left pending and moves it to tomorrow.

Just say *"I want to change X"* and it adjusts it.
