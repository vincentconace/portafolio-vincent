## What changes

If you pay for Facebook Ads, you know Mondays. You sit down to:
- Review what happened last week (1h refreshing Ads Manager)
- Check what competitors are doing (Ads Library, 30 min)
- Create new creatives (1-3h depending on the team)
- Upload them to Ads Manager (30 min)
- Set up the A/B test (30 min)

**5-6 hours every Monday. Forever.**

Cloud Ads Flow tackles that: **3 systems working together** inside Claude so the entire loop repeats itself.

| Piece | What it does |
|-------|--------------|
| **Cowork** (built-in Claude.ai) | Reads the Ads Library, identifies niche winners |
| **[Higgsfield MCP](/boveda/higgsfield-mcp)** | Generates 10 variants of the winning creative with your brand |
| **Facebook MCP** | Uploads the creatives to Ads Manager and turns on A/B |

## The 5 moves

### Move 1 — Scan the Ads Library

```
> Go to the Facebook Ads Library, filter:
  - Country: Argentina
  - Niche: "SaaS pricing tools" or "B2B revenue ops"
  - Ads active for >14 days (a signal they work)

  Pull the 10 most likely winners for me. For each one:
  - Screenshot of the creative
  - Copy text
  - Time active
  - Spend estimate (if the Ads Library shows it)
  - Why you think it works (hook + angle)
```

Output: docs with the niche's 10 winning ads.

### Move 2 — Identify the winning pattern

```
> From the 10 you pulled, identify the common pattern.
  - What kind of hook dominates?
  - Most used format (photo / video / carousel)?
  - What pain points do they mention?
  - What CTAs do they use?

  Give me the niche's "winning formula" in 1 page.
```

You don't copy the ads (that would be plagiarism + Meta detects it). **You extract the formula** to apply with your brand.

### Move 3 — Generate variants with your brand

```
> Higgsfield: with the formula from step 2, generate 10 variants for
  my product.

  My brand:
  - Product: [...]
  - Audience: [...]
  - Palette: [...]
  - Tone: [...]
  - Tagline: [...]

  Variants that differ in:
  - 3 with hook A (frustration)
  - 3 with hook B (curiosity)
  - 4 with hook C (result)

  Output: 10 images / videos ready for Ads Manager.
```

Details of the Higgsfield workflow in [Higgsfield MCP](/boveda/higgsfield-mcp).

### Move 4 — Upload to Facebook Ads Manager

```
> Facebook MCP: upload the 10 ads to a new campaign.

  Settings:
  - Objective: conversions (signup)
  - Budget: $20/day per ad set
  - Audience: [...]
  - Pixel: [your pixel ID]
  - Destination URL: [your landing]

  Set up 70/30 A/B testing between the hooks. The 10 ads in
  separate ad sets by hook.

  BEFORE turning on: show me the final review.
```

It shows you the configuration. You confirm. They go live.

### Move 5 — Weekly loop

[Scheduled Cowork task](/boveda/nueve-prompts-cowork) that runs every Monday:

```
Every Monday 8am:

1. Review ad set performance over the last 7 days
2. Identify:
   - Winners (low CPA, high ROAS)
   - Losers (high CPA, no conversion)
3. Pause the losers
4. For the winners: ask Higgsfield for 5 more variants
5. Upload variants to a new ad set
6. Tell me which one was the winner of the week
```

**The Monday you used to spend refreshing Ads Manager** becomes reviewing the report the agent left for you.

## Critical anti-patterns

### 1. Copying competitors' ads literally

Taking a competitor's creative and uploading it as-is = **copyright violation**. Meta also detects it and bans your account.

**Only extract the formula** (structure, hooks, angles). Your visual and copy have to be **your own**.

### 2. Launching 50 ads with no method

10 well-tested ads > 50 random ads. **Discipline over volume**.

### 3. Not auditing the setup periodically

After 4-8 weeks, **full audit**:
- Did the winning formula change?
- Are your best ads still performing or did they burn out from frequency?
- Have new competitors with relevant ads appeared?

Without an audit, the loop becomes mechanical and stops adapting.

### 4. Not enough budget for learning

Each ad set needs **at least 50 conversions** for Meta to learn. If your CPA is $50, that's $2500 per ad set. **If you don't have that budget, the A/Bs won't yield useful data**.

### 5. Trusting 100% what the agent decides

The agent proposes, **you validate**. Pausing an ad without reviewing can pause one that was about to take off the following week. **Approve the critical decisions**.

## Combining with a monthly manual audit

Once a month, a full audit with [Claude Ads (part of the marketing agency)](/boveda/agencia-marketing-claude):

```
> /claude-ads audit my entire Facebook Ads account for the last month.

  Cover:
  - Creative fatigue
  - Targeting overlap
  - Inefficient bidding
  - Pixel firing correctly
  - Conversion attribution

  Give me the top 5 critical issues + recommendations.
```

The weekly loop keeps the engine running; the monthly audit prevents accumulated drift.

## When this flow wins

✅ You manage your own Facebook Ads (not an agency)
✅ Monthly spend > $1500 (justifies the setup)
✅ You accept reviewing 1h per week (it's not 0 work)
✅ Your product has clear, trackable conversion

❌ Very low spend (less than $300/month) → no data for A/B testing
❌ Niche with strict regulation (gambling, health, finance) → Meta rejects creative ads
❌ You already have an agency doing this well → duplicate effort

## The realistic math

Time saved per week: **4-5 hours**.
Additional cost vs doing it by hand:
- Higgsfield: $30-100/month
- Facebook MCP: included in the paid plan
- Cowork: your existing Claude plan

If your hour is worth more than $20, **it pays for itself in a week**.

What it does NOT save:
- The judgment to decide which ads to scale / pause (yours)
- The overall budget decisions (yours)
- The campaign strategy (yours)
