## What it solves

WhatsApp is the #1 channel for support and sales in LATAM. But handling it seriously requires:

- Being available for extended hours
- Responding fast (leads cool off in hours)
- Keeping a consistent tone across operators
- Documenting the history of every conversation

For small businesses without a large team, this **doesn't scale**. **WhatsApp AgentKit** (open source, MIT) solves that: in ~30 min you set up an agent that responds 24/7, using your Claude Code as the engine.

> **Important warning**: just like [OpenWA](/boveda/openwa-whatsapp), this uses an UNOFFICIAL WhatsApp connection → risk of the number getting banned. Read the "Hard rules" section before you start.

## What you build

In the end you have a system with 3 pieces:

1. **WhatsApp Gateway**: connects to your number (via QR like WhatsApp Web)
2. **Bridge**: receives messages, passes them to Claude, returns the response
3. **Knowledge base**: what the agent "knows" about your business (products, FAQs, policies)

Plus optional:
4. **Human handoff**: when the agent can't handle it, it alerts you
5. **Logs**: conversation history for audit and improvement

## The setup

### The 3 things the kit will ask you

Claude builds almost everything. You just answer:

<Step number={1} title="About your business">

```
- What do you sell / what do you do? (1 paragraph)
- Who is your typical customer?
- Which products / services? (list)
- Prices?
- Return / warranty policy?
- Support hours (when do you respond as a human)?
- What should the agent NOT answer? (e.g., legal matters, unauthorized discounts)
```

This info is what the agent will "know." The more complete it is, the better it responds.

</Step>

<Step number={2} title="About the tone">

```
- How do you talk to your customers? (formal / friendly / casual)
- Do you use "vos" / "tú" / "usted"?
- Are there words you NEVER use?
- Are there typical phrases you DO use?
- Share 3-5 examples of real conversations (from WhatsApp Business)
```

From the examples, Claude learns your voice. Without examples, it sounds generic.

</Step>

<Step number={3} title="About escalation to a human" last>

```
- When MUST the agent hand the case over to you?
  - Purchases > $X
  - Serious complaints
  - Questions it can't answer
  - VIP customer (list)
- How does it notify you? (another WhatsApp, Slack, email)
- How does it explain to the customer that "someone will get back to you"?
```

</Step>

With those 3 sections filled in, Claude Code builds the code and the agent is up and running.

## What happens on day 1

Your first day with the agent:

### The first conversations

```
[Customer]: Hi, do you sell product X?
[Agent]: Hi there! Yes, we have it. It costs $Y. Would you like me
         to send you the full catalog?

[Customer]: Do you ship to Córdoba?
[Agent]: Yes, we ship nationwide. It reaches Córdoba in 3-5
         business days. Want to get a quote for one?

[Customer]: My order #4521 hasn't arrived.
[Agent]: I see order #4521 is marked as in transit. I'm going to
         hand this case over to someone on the team to look into
         it. They'll contact you in a few minutes.
[Agent → you via Slack]: Order #4521 with a delivery problem.
         Customer: Juan Pérez. WhatsApp: +54...
```

### What you'll see on day 1

✅ Correct answers for clear FAQs
✅ Escalation to you in the right cases
⚠️ Some "I didn't quite get that, could you rephrase?" when the customer writes ambiguously
⚠️ Some tone errors (not exactly how you talk)
❌ Some cases where it could have answered but didn't

**You'll iterate during the first week**: every time the agent fails, **you add context to the knowledge base**. After 2-3 weeks, it's dialed in.

## The hard rules (same warning as OpenWA)

> **NEVER connect your personal number.** If it gets banned, you lose your personal WhatsApp. Use a **new dedicated number**.

> **DON'T send mass outbound.** The kit is for **responding**, not for sending cold spam. Mass outbound gets your number banned fast.

> **Don't automate replies to sensitive cases.** Serious complaints, legal matters, regulatory issues → always a human.

> **Present it as an "agent" if it helps.** Some businesses say at the start: "I'm [agent name], the AI assistant for [company]. If you need a human, tell me and I'll connect you." More transparent, less risk of a complaint about "being tricked."

## Cases where it shines

### 1. E-commerce with repetitive inquiries

90% of the questions are the same:
- Do you ship to X?
- How much is Y?
- When will my order arrive?
- Do you accept cards?

The agent answers them instantly. You only handle the 10% that needs a human.

### 2. Professional services with triage

You're a lawyer / accountant / consultant. The agent:
- Qualifies whether the inquiry is for you or not
- Asks for basic info (name, problem, urgency)
- Passes you only the qualified cases

You filter out the 60% of inquiries that aren't your fit.

### 3. Restaurants / delivery

Takes basic orders, confirms hours, sends the menu. **Only escalates to a human when there's something non-standard.**

### 4. Education / courses

FAQs about the program, enrollment, costs. Drafts answers for more complex questions (which a human validates before sending).

## What it doesn't do well

❌ **Negotiation**: the agent closes at the listed price. If someone asks for a discount, it should escalate.
❌ **Deep technical inquiries**: if the customer asks things that require expertise, **escalate**.
❌ **Intense emotions**: angry customer, serious complaint → always a human.
❌ **Complex multi-step**: processes with many variables → a web form is better.

## Pattern: agent + transparent human handoff

The common mistake is presenting the agent as a human. People notice → they get annoyed.

A better pattern:

```
[Customer]: Hi
[Agent]: Hi there! I'm Sofi, the AI assistant for [Company]. I
         help with frequently asked questions 24/7. If you need
         to talk to a human, just say "human agent" at any time.
         How can I help you?

[Customer]: What are the prices for the kits?
[Agent]: [responds with prices]

[Customer]: human agent
[Agent]: Great, I'll pass your message to the team. They'll
         contact you during office hours (Monday to Friday, 9 to 18).
[Agent → you]: Customer wants to talk to you. Context: [...].
```

**Transparent. Functional. No complaints generated.**

## Useful combinations

### With a CRM (HubSpot / Pipedrive)

When a new lead comes in, the agent:
1. Responds instantly
2. Loads it into the CRM with the extracted info
3. If it qualified as "warm/hot," assigns it to you in the CRM

Zero data loss between WhatsApp and the CRM.

### With ManyChat for a more structured flow

For flows like "initial inquiry → catalog → cart → checkout," ManyChat can handle the structured flow, and the AI agent only shows up for free-form questions.

### With [managed agents](/boveda/agentes-managed)

If you don't want to maintain your own server: run the agent's logic as a Managed Agent. You pay only per active minute, no server.

---

## When NOT to use this pattern

❌ Your volume is very low (~10 messages/day) → handling it by hand is enough
❌ Your business can't tolerate the risk of the number getting banned
❌ Regulated industry (health, finance, legal advice) → the official WhatsApp Business API is better
❌ Your margin can't absorb an occasional agent error
