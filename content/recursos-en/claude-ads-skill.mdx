## What it does

An open-source skill that turns Claude Code into a **paid media auditor** for 3 platforms:

- Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)
- Google Ads (Search + Display + Performance Max)
- TikTok Ads

It runs **161 severity-weighted checks** on your account, across **3 parallel agents** (one per platform), and returns an **Ads Health Score 0–100** with an A–F grade.

It's the "audit + coach" version of the marketing workflow — it complements what you built in [Cloud Ads Flow](/boveda/cloud-ads-flow) and [Andromeda — 8 Meta Ads rules](/boveda/andromeda-meta-ads).

---

## The 2 modes

### `/ads start` — initial wizard

The first time you invoke the skill:

```
> /ads start

[Skill]
  Hi. I'm going to audit your accounts. I need:

  1. Which platforms do you use?
     [x] Meta  [x] Google  [x] TikTok

  2. For each platform:
     - What's your ad account ID?
     - Do you have a token configured?
     - What's your main objective? (conversion/leads/awareness)
     - What's your target KPI? (CPA, ROAS, CTR)

  3. Your business category:
     [you pick from a list]

  4. Your experience level with each platform:
     - Meta: beginner/intermediate/advanced
     - Google: ...
     - TikTok: ...
```

With that, it sets everything up and runs a **first full audit**.

### `/ads next` — continuous coach

Once configured, the second mode is a coach:

```
> /ads next

[Skill]
  Last audit: 7 days ago. Score: 67 (grade C+)

  Changes since then:
  - You paused 3 ad sets (good, I'd flagged them as bad)
  - You uploaded 4 new creatives (under evaluation)
  - 2 new active campaigns

  Priority actions for this week:

  🔴 Critical:
  - Campaign "Lookalike-1pct" has CPA 3.2× target. Pause it NOW.

  🟡 High:
  - 3 ad sets with frequency >4.5 (audience fatigue). Refresh creative.

  🟢 Improvable:
  - Missing event match quality optimization in Meta Pixel.

  Want me to dig into any of these?
```

It's **continuous, not one-shot**. You run it every Monday (or whenever you prefer) and it keeps tracking your progress.

## The 161 checks (summary by category)

| Category | How many checks | Examples |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Creative** | 35 | Hookrate, frequency, fatigue, A/B coverage |
| **Targeting** | 28 | Audience size, overlap, exclusions |
| **Bidding** | 22 | Strategy fit, budget pacing, cap optimization |
| **Tracking** | 24 | Pixel events, attribution, conversion API |
| **Compliance** | 18 | Prohibited terms, disclaimers, country restrictions |
| **Performance** | 19 | CPA trends, ROAS, frequency, CTR |
| **Account health** | 15 | Permissions, billing, status, holds |

**Weighted by severity**: a critical check (e.g. broken pixel) counts more than a medium one (e.g. inconsistent naming).

## The Ads Health Score 0-100

| Score | Grade | Status |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 90-100 | A+ / A | Excellent. Fine-tuning. |
| 75-89 | B+ / B | Good. There's room but it's solid. |
| 60-74 | C+ / C | Average. Clear issues to tackle. |
| 40-59 | D+ / D | Problematic. Several areas compromised. |
| 0-39 | F | Critical. Reset / rethink needed. |

**The point of the score**: it gives you an objective number to track progress month over month.

Month 1: 52 (D)
Month 2: 67 (C+)
Month 3: 78 (B)

You know you're improving. Without a score, "I'm getting better" is subjective.

## The 3 parallel agents

What sets this skill apart: it uses **sub-agents** to audit the 3 platforms **in parallel**:

```
[Your prompt: /ads audit]
   ↓
[Orchestrator]
   ├── [Meta Agent] — runs 35+ Meta checks
   ├── [Google Agent] — runs 35+ Google checks
   └── [TikTok Agent] — runs 35+ TikTok checks
   ↓
[Orchestrator consolidates]
   ↓
[Unified report]
```

**Speed**: sequential would be 6-9 minutes. Parallel: 2-3 minutes.

## Read-only mode by default

As with [Claude Meta Ads](/boveda/claude-meta-ads-setup):

- By default it only reads
- For changes you need an explicit `WRITE_MODE=true`
- For critical changes it asks for confirmation

**Recommendation**: use the skill **in read-only mode only** for 1-2 months. Once you trust what it detects, consider giving it write access for mechanical changes (pausing ads with high CPA, for example).

## Use cases

### 1. Freelance audit

New client. They give you access to their accounts. **In 1 hour**:

```
> /ads start
[you configure the accounts]
> /ads audit deep

[2-3 minutes]

[Full report across all 3 platforms]
```

You turn the report into a services proposal:
- "Your Meta has a score of 45 (D). Here are the top 5 fixes that would push it to 75 (B+)."

You close the sale with an objective argument.

### 2. Weekly coach for your own account

Every Monday 9am, `/ads next` runs as a [scheduled task](/boveda/nueve-prompts-cowork):

- It reminds you of critical issues
- It compares against the previous week
- It proposes 3 actions for the week

### 3. Junior onboarding

A junior marketer joins the team. Instead of explaining everything:

```
> /ads explain [specific issue]

[Skill explains the problem, why it matters, how to fix it]
```

They learn from the audit by doing, not from generic courses.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Expecting it to replace strategy

The skill audits **execution**. It doesn't define **strategy**. It tells you "this is technically wrong"; it doesn't tell you "your product is poorly positioned in the market".

### 2. Attacking EVERYTHING at once

If your score is 45, there are 50 things wrong. **Don't fix them all at once**. Tackle the critical ones first. Then the high ones. Changing 30 things simultaneously = you don't know what moved the needle.

### 3. Score as the only metric

The score is a useful proxy. **It's not the end goal**. Your goal is low CAC, high ROAS, growing revenue. The score correlates but isn't the same thing.

### 4. Skipping the coach

`/ads start` is a one-time thing. `/ads next` is the one that delivers continuous value. **Don't stop at the initial audit**.

## What the skill does NOT do

❌ **Create ads automatically**: it only audits. For creation, check out [Claude Meta Ads setup](/boveda/claude-meta-ads-setup) or [Cloud Ads Flow](/boveda/cloud-ads-flow).

❌ **Optimize bid strategies for you**: it tells you if your strategy is suboptimal, but you make the change yourself.

❌ **Guess business criteria**: if your target CPA is $20 but your LTV is $400, no algorithm will know that unless you feed it in.

❌ **Replace best practices**: if you've never run ads, read [Andromeda — 8 rules](/boveda/andromeda-meta-ads) first.

## When it's worth it

✅ You spend >$2k/month on paid media (it justifies the setup)
✅ You run >2 platforms simultaneously
✅ You want to improve consistently but don't have a framework
✅ You're a freelancer auditing client accounts

❌ You're just starting out (no data to audit)
❌ Very low spend
❌ A mature paid media team with its own frameworks
