## What it is

BrowserAct is a cloud browser automation platform focused on structured data extraction. What sets it apart from [Apify](/boveda/mcp-apify) and [Bright Data](/boveda/mcp-brightdata) is its coverage of the Asian market: in addition to Amazon, YouTube, Google Maps and Reddit, it supports WeChat and Zhihu — core platforms in China that other scrapers don't touch.

If your use case needs data from mainstream Chinese platforms, or you want an alternative to Apify/Bright Data focused on structured data (not raw HTML), this MCP is the one to reach for.

## How to install it

```bash
npx claude-code-templates@latest --mcp web-data/browseract
```

You'll need a BrowserAct account and API key.

For Claude:

```
Install the web-data/browseract MCP. Let's set the credentials and get me
the top 10 products for an Amazon search for [keyword], with
price, rating and review count in JSON format.
```

## What you can do with this

### E-commerce intelligence

"Show me the top 50 products on Amazon US for `mechanical keyboard`, sorted by reviews, with price and rating." Output already structured, not raw scraping.

### Market research in China

"Find me Zhihu conversations about `<a topic>` with more than 100 upvotes in the last 6 months." Accessible data that doesn't show up in other tools.

### Trends on YouTube

"Pull the data from the last 30 videos on channel X: views, likes, comments, publish date." For performance analysis.

### Local business intelligence

"Restaurants in Buenos Aires with a rating above 4.5 and more than 200 reviews, sorted by most recent review." A Maps + News + Reddit combination.

### Compared with alternatives

- **vs [Apify](/boveda/mcp-apify):** Apify has a broader catalog (5000+ Actors) and a free tier. BrowserAct is more focused but covers WeChat/Zhihu.
- **vs [Bright Data](/boveda/mcp-brightdata):** Bright Data is enterprise-grade with 60+ tools. BrowserAct is lighter and more affordably priced.

## Prerequisites

- **An active BrowserAct account**
- **API token** from the dashboard
- **Use cases that fit** the sites it covers (it doesn't scrape any URL)

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **For sites outside the catalog:** BrowserAct covers specific platforms. If you need to scrape a random site, [Bright Data Web Unlocker](/boveda/mcp-brightdata) or [browser-use](/boveda/mcp-browser-use-mcp-server) are better.
- **If you need enterprise scale:** Bright Data is still the serious option for very high volume.
- **If your use is indie and low-volume:** Apify's free tier may be enough.
- **WeChat/Zhihu — regulatory caveat:** scraping Chinese platforms carries specific regulation. Check it.
- **24 installs is low:** less community testing = fewer known issues. Try it in a sandbox before relying on it seriously.

</Callout>
