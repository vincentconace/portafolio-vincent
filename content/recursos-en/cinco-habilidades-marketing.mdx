## The premise

A decent marketing department costs **$8-15k/month**. A good freelance copywriter charges $3-5k. A professional SEO audit runs $500-5000. A video editor asks for $50-150/hour.

5 open source skills installed in Claude Code give you a **functional equivalent of 80% of that department**, for free. They don't replace a senior human — but they cover the 80% of mechanical work you were going to delegate anyway.

The 5:

| Skill | Role | What for |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **Marketing Skills** | Copywriter | 34 sub-skills: copy, emails, ads, pricing |
| **Claude SEO** | SEO auditor | Audits your site, optimizes for Google + AI |
| **Brand Guidelines** | Brand director | Applies your visual identity to everything it generates |
| **Editor Pro Max** | Video editor | You describe the video → Remotion builds it |
| **NotebookLM Skill** | Research assistant | Connects Claude to your real docs (no hallucinating) |

## Skill 1 — Marketing Skills

### What it is

34 specialized sub-skills, each trained for a distinct marketing workflow:

- Copy for landing pages (long-form sales letters, hero copy)
- Email marketing (welcome flows, broadcast, re-engagement)
- Paid ads (Meta Ads, Google Ads, creatives)
- Pricing strategy (anchoring, tiers, discounts)
- Retention (referrals, loyalty, win-back)
- Organic growth (SEO content, social posts)

When you ask Claude for something marketing-related, **the skill automatically matches** the right sub-skill and applies the method.

### Installation

```bash
npx skills add coreyhaines31/marketingskills
```

### When it shines

- You need decent copy without hiring a copywriter
- Your product is new and you have no reference for what tone to use
- You want to A/B multiple versions (Marketing Skills gives you 5-10 variants in one pass)

### When NOT

- You have a senior copywriter who already knows your brand inside out (you'll over-correct whatever Claude delivers)
- Super technical/niche product where the generic copywriter doesn't get the nuances
- You need a very specific voice (there [Brand Guidelines](#skill-3) matters more than Marketing Skills alone)

## Skill 2 — Claude SEO

### What it is

19 sub-skills for SEO auditing and optimization. Main command:

```
/seo audit https://miapp.com
```

And Claude:

1. Scrapes your site
2. Audits meta tags, schema, speed, broken links
3. Detects keywords you already rank for and new opportunities
4. Optimizes for **AI Overviews** (Google) + answer engines like Perplexity
5. Gives you a report prioritized by impact/effort

### Installation

```bash
git clone https://github.com/AgriciDaniel/claude-seo.git
cd claude-seo
# Follow the README to activate it as a local skill
```

### When it shines

- You just launched and want an SEO baseline
- Your site is 6+ months old and you've never audited it
- You want to optimize so AIs (ChatGPT, Perplexity) cite you

### When NOT

- Enterprise site with 50k+ URLs (Claude SEO doesn't scale well, you need Screaming Frog + Ahrefs)
- If you already pay for SEMrush/Ahrefs and use them well, Claude SEO is redundant for auditing — but **it is useful** for AI-first optimization

### Pairs well with

[Sentry MCP plugin](/boveda/plugin-sentry-mcp) if you have 5xx errors killing your crawl budget.

## Skill 3 — Brand Guidelines

### What it is

An **official Anthropic skill**. You load your brand manual once (colors, typography, tone, do/don'ts) and Claude applies it automatically every time it generates visual or textual content.

### Why it matters more than it seems

Without Brand Guidelines, every Claude generation (presentation, banner, email) is **independent**. You have to keep repeating "use hex #FF6B35", "this typeface", "this tone". Over 10 generations, your brand looks fragmented.

With Brand Guidelines, **one initial load** and Claude respects everything: colors in CSS, typefaces in Figma exports, tone in copy, layouts in presentations.

### Installation

```bash
claude install-skill https://github.com/anthropics/skills/tree/main/skills/brand-guidelines
```

Then you build your brand pack:

```
> Load the brand pack from /docs/brand/. Activate the skill for
  this whole session.
```

### When it shines

- Established brand with a visual guide
- You produce a lot of content (LinkedIn, blogs, ads) and need consistency
- Distributed team — everyone uses Claude but all respect the same identity

### When NOT

- Brand still in exploration (you don't have colors/typography defined yet)
- You produce 1-2 pieces a month (the setup isn't worth it)

## Skill 4 — Editor Pro Max

### What it is

Generates videos by describing them in natural language. Under the hood it uses **Remotion** (a React framework for programmatic video). 25 pre-built components, 10 templates (TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, presentations).

### The flow

```
> I need a 30s video for LinkedIn. Hook: "If your team doesn't use
  AI, you lose 10h/week". Show me 3 stats with numbers animating
  up. Final CTA: "Try Claude for free". My brand logo at the
  end.
```

Claude:
1. Selects a template
2. Applies the brand pack if you have [Brand Guidelines](#skill-3) installed
3. Generates the Remotion code
4. Renders the video locally
5. Delivers a ready-to-use `.mp4`

### Installation

```bash
git clone https://github.com/Hainrixz/editor-pro-max.git
cd editor-pro-max
npm install
```

### When it shines

- You produce short video in volume (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
- Your content is "talking head with stats + text animation" (the standard educational content format)
- You have Whisper locally for automatic subtitles

### When NOT

- You need real cinematic video → better to use [Higgsfield MCP](/boveda/higgsfield-mcp) with Seedance 2.0
- You want creative editing with lots of real footage (Editor Pro Max is for programmatic video, not traditional editing)
- Enterprise production where you need client approval on every frame (programmatic adds friction there)

## Skill 5 — NotebookLM Skill

### What it is

Connects Claude to **Google NotebookLM** — your collection of PDFs, videos, docs. When you ask questions, Claude **queries your real sources** instead of making things up.

### The problem it solves

LLMs **hallucinate**. You ask something specific to your industry and it throws out data that looks real but is wrong. With NotebookLM Skill, Claude:

1. Reads your question
2. Searches your NotebookLM notebooks
3. Extracts real quotes with references
4. Answers based **only** on what's in your docs

Zero hallucination. If it's not in your docs, it says "I can't find it".

### Installation

```bash
mkdir -p ~/.claude/skills
git clone https://github.com/PleasePrompto/notebooklm-skill ~/.claude/skills/notebooklm
```

The first time it asks you to auth with Google (just once).

### When it shines

- You have a lot of internal documentation (policies, manuals, contracts)
- You do academic/legal research where **exact citations are critical**
- Regulated industry (medical, legal, finance) where "hallucinating" is unacceptable

### When NOT

- Your workflow is 100% creative (there are no "sources" to consult)
- Documentation lives in non-Google systems (Confluence, private Notion) — there you need specific MCPs like [Notion MCP](/boveda/plugin-notion-mcp)

## How they work together — the full flow

The real value **isn't each skill in isolation**. It's the combined flow:

```
1. Idea → Marketing Skills generates strategy + copy
2. Copy → Brand Guidelines applies voice and colors
3. Web → Claude SEO audits and optimizes
4. Promo video → Editor Pro Max produces the clip
5. Verification → NotebookLM consults your real data to avoid errors
```

### Concrete example

You want to launch a campaign for a new product:

```
> My product: inventory management SaaS for mid-sized LATAM retail.
  Launch in 2 weeks. Brand pack loaded in /docs/brand/.
  Market data in my NotebookLM "retail-latam-research".

  Build me the full campaign:

  1. Marketing Skills: strategy + 5 emails for the launch sequence
  2. Brand Guidelines: apply colors and tone across everything
  3. NotebookLM: validate that my claims about the market are backed up
  4. Editor Pro Max: 1 30s video for LinkedIn
  5. Claude SEO: review my current landing page and optimize it

  Deliver a consolidated brief with everything.
```

A single Claude session → almost the entire campaign ready for review.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Installing them all at once without understanding what each does

If you don't know what each skill does, **you'll misuse the output**. Install one, use it for 1-2 weeks, then the next.

### 2. Not loading Brand Guidelines before requesting content

If you ask "build me a sales email" without Brand Guidelines active, Claude uses a generic default. **The email will be correct but it won't sound like your brand**.

### 3. Blindly trusting Marketing Skills

34 sub-skills sounds impressive but **they're generalist frameworks**. If your product is very niche, the default copy will sound plastic. Iterate with human feedback.

### 4. NotebookLM with outdated docs

If your notebook has info from 2024, Claude will answer with that info even if it no longer applies today. **Curate your sources** periodically.

### 5. Editor Pro Max for video that needs real emotion

Programmatic video looks **correct** but not **emotional**. For brand-driven campaigns where emotion is the ROI, don't automate.
