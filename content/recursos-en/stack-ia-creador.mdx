## The problem

If you produce content with AI, within a few weeks you end up with **8-12 tools open in tabs**, each with its own login, its own separate credits, its own prompt style. Managing the pile takes more time than producing content.

What follows is an **opinionated stack** of 6 tools that cover the creator's full cycle, **chained together by a master prompt** in Claude that orchestrates the rest.

> Note: this is a specific stack. If your workflow is different (more editorial, less video, etc.), adapt it. The structure — tools + orchestrator — works even if you swap out every piece.

---

## The end-to-end flow

```
┌─ Master Prompt (Claude) ─┐
│                            │
│   Genera prompts para:     │
│                            │
│   1. Guion ────────► Claude (Opus + Sonnet)
│   2. Imagen ───────► Nano Banana Pro / GPT Image
│   3. Video ────────► Veo 3.1 / SeeDance 2 / Higgsfield
│   4. Voz ──────────► ElevenLabs
│   5. Avatar ───────► HeyGen
│   6. DMs ──────────► ManyChat
│                            │
└────────────────────────────┘
```

You give the brief to the master prompt. It generates the **specific prompts** each tool needs, in the format each one expects. You copy-paste into each tool.

## The 6 tools

### 1. Script — Claude (Opus 4.7 + Sonnet 4.6)

For writing the content script:

- **Sonnet** for quick drafts, simple iterations
- **Opus** for complex scripts, elaborate hooks, non-obvious structure

**Why Claude and not another**: reasoning about content structure (hook, development, payoff) tends to be stronger. And it keeps your voice if you feed it examples.

For best results: build yourself a "voice writer" skill trained on your own writing (see [JARVIS in Obsidian](/boveda/jarvis-obsidian)).

### 2. Image — Nano Banana Pro + ChatGPT Image

For static visuals:

- **Nano Banana Pro** for photoreal and editorial
- **GPT Image** for stylized variants, illustration

**Which one, when**: if you need a realistic person or a product photo → Nano Banana. If you need illustration / a non-photographic style → GPT Image.

To integrate it into your Claude flow, check out [Higgsfield MCP](/boveda/higgsfield-mcp), which brings image models right into the chat.

### 3. Video — Veo 3.1 / SeeDance 2

Generative cinematic video:

- **Veo 3.1** for realistic clips with complex motion
- **SeeDance 2** for natural movement, physics

**Which one, when**: it depends on the type of movement. For scenes with people → SeeDance (better at human motion). For environments / landscapes / products → Veo (better at cinematic composition).

### 4. Voice — ElevenLabs

For voiceover and narration:

- Cloned voices (your own voice or an actor's)
- 30+ languages with native-level quality
- Fine control over emotion and tone

**Typical workflow**:
1. Claude writes the script
2. You paste it into ElevenLabs
3. You pick a voice
4. You adjust pacing/emotion if needed
5. You download the MP3

If you're going to do a lot of this, you can clone **your own voice** and use it as a consistent author.

### 5. Avatar — HeyGen

To create a talking head of an avatar (your face or a generated one):

- You clone your face once
- You feed it text + audio (from ElevenLabs)
- HeyGen lipsyncs with your avatar
- Output: a video of "you" talking

**When it shines**: educational content, talking heads for LinkedIn/YouTube Shorts, course presenters.

**When NOT to**: if your authenticity of "being there" is part of your brand value, faking "your face" when it isn't really you feels off.

### 6. DMs — ManyChat

Not generative AI — it's **automation for Instagram/Facebook/WhatsApp DMs**.

The last step of the creator flow: someone comments on your post, ManyChat sends them a DM with the lead magnet, and pulls them into your list.

Connected to Claude via webhooks, Claude can personalize replies based on each lead's context.

## The master prompt

What ties the stack together. A **skill in Claude** that knows:

- Your niche / audience / brand
- The format each tool expects (Nano Banana, Veo, ElevenLabs, etc.)
- How to chain outputs (script → image prompt → video prompt)

Example skill:

```markdown
---
name: stack-creator
description: Master prompt para producción de contenido. Activate cuando
  pida producir reel/post/short/video. Encadena guion → visuals → audio.
---

# Stack Creator

When I ask you to produce content, do the following:

## Step 1 — Understand the brief
Before generating anything, ask me for:
- Platform (TikTok / Reels / YouTube Short / LinkedIn / etc.)
- Target duration (seconds)
- Goal (awareness / conversion / educate / entertain)
- Hook style (if I want something specific)
- Exact audience

## Step 2 — Generate the script
With Sonnet/Opus depending on complexity. Format:

[FRAME 0-3s]: hook (visual description + audio + on-screen text)
[FRAME 3-X]: development (each beat with visuals and voiceover)
[FRAME X-end]: payoff + CTA

Voice: my learned voice (check examples in ~/voz/).

## Step 3 — Generate prompts for each tool

For each element of the script, generate the specific prompt:

### For image (Nano Banana Pro)
"[dense visual description, photoreal style, 9:16 vertical, lighting X,
mood Y, character details, no text overlay]"

### For video (Veo / SeeDance)
"[motion description, camera movement, duration, transitions]"

### For voice (ElevenLabs)
"[voiceover text with no formatting, one line per frame]"
"Voice: my-cloned-voice
Emotion: [calm/excited/serious]"

### For avatar (HeyGen) - only if applicable
"Avatar: my-avatar
Script: [text to speak]"

## Step 4 — Summarize the flow

Give me back a structured doc with:
1. The full script
2. Prompts for each tool (copy-paste ready)
3. Production order (what I do first, what second)
4. Total estimated time

## Rules

- If I'm torn between Veo and SeeDance, recommend one with a reason
- If the content doesn't need one of the 6 (e.g. it doesn't need an avatar),
  skip it explicitly
- My tone: [define your tone]
- Constraints: [define what not to do]
```

With this skill, **a 3-line brief** turns into 6 prompts ready for 6 tools, in 30 seconds.

## Real workflow — producing a reel

```
[Master Prompt]
> Reel para Instagram. 30 segundos. Sobre el problema típico de
  founders SaaS: gastar plata en growth antes de tener product-market
  fit. Audiencia: founders early-stage LATAM.

[Master Prompt te genera:]

GUION:
[FRAME 0-3s]: Hook visual: founder mirando dashboard con números
  bajos, cara de frustración. Texto: "Gasté $50k en Meta ads."
[FRAME 3-15s]: Desarrollo: voiceover explica que sin PMF, el budget
  acelera la caída. Visual: gráfico de embudo con leak en el medio.
[FRAME 15-25s]: Solución: validar PMF primero. Visual: pre-PMF
  checklist.
[FRAME 25-30s]: CTA: "Hablo con 5 founders gratis esta semana, link
  en bio."

PROMPTS GENERADOS:

🖼 IMAGEN (Nano Banana Pro):
"Founder argentino 35, en su escritorio, mirando laptop con expresión
de frustración. Iluminación natural de ventana. Cinemático, 9:16,
photoreal."

🎬 VIDEO (Veo):
"Subtle camera push-in toward laptop screen. Founder slowly turns to
look at camera, defeated expression. 3 seconds, 9:16."

🎙 VOZ (ElevenLabs):
"Gasté cincuenta mil dólares en publicidad antes de tener product-market
fit. ¿Qué pasó? El embudo seguía leakeando. Cuando no tenés PMF, el
budget no escala el negocio — acelera la caída."
Voice: mi-voz-clonada
Emotion: reflective

🎭 AVATAR (HeyGen): no aplica este reel (usás el visual del founder)

📩 DM (ManyChat trigger):
Si comenta "MENTORÍA" → DM: "Hola [nombre], 5 founders por semana,
agendá acá: [link]"

ORDEN:
1. Generar imagen (1 min)
2. Animar con Veo (3 min)
3. Voz en ElevenLabs (2 min)
4. Editar en CapCut con voz + video (10 min)
5. Subir + configurar ManyChat trigger (5 min)

TOTAL: ~25 min para reel completo.
```

Without the stack and the master prompt: that reel takes 2-3 hours.

## Anti-patterns

### 1. A stack with no master prompt

If you have the 6 tools but you type the prompts by hand every time, **you lose 80% of the value**. The master prompt is what ties the pieces together.

### 2. Too many tools

6 is already a lot. If you add another 4 "for completeness," the overhead kills you. **Start with fewer** (3-4), add more when you notice the gap.

### 3. Not cloning your voice

ElevenLabs with a generic voice sounds like AI. With **your cloned voice**, it sounds like you. The difference is night and day for authenticity.

### 4. An avatar for everything

HeyGen is good but detectable. For content where authenticity matters, use your real face. Use an avatar for mass educational pieces, not for your personal brand reel.

## When this stack makes sense

✅ You produce visual content frequently (weekly+)
✅ Your business depends on consistent output
✅ You have the budget for the credits (it's not free)
✅ You're willing to iterate to reach good output

❌ Occasional content → it's overkill
❌ If your value is "being there live" → fake content doesn't apply
❌ If your niche rejects visible AI content → evaluate your audience first
