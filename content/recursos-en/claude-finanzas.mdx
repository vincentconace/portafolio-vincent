## What it is

Anthropic released **Claude Financial Services**: a layer of Claude built for people who live in Excel, PowerPoint, and reports — bankers, analysts, controllers, or any business owner who deals with close, budgeting, and valuations.

It comes as **6 pre-built agents** inside Claude Cowork. There's nothing to train — you turn them on like a plug-in.

| Agent | What it's for |
|--------|----------|
| **Pitch Books** | Investor presentations with valuations and comparables already formatted |
| **Credit Memos** | Complete credit memo with DSCR, LTV, risk flags |
| **KYC** | Structured Know Your Customer file |
| **Ledger Reconciliation** | Account and general ledger reconciliation |
| **Actuarial Review** | Review of actuarial models |
| **Monthly Close** | End-to-end monthly close |

The key thing: **they work on your real files**. They don't export, they don't redo. They edit the Excel cells and leave a visible audit trail so you can review before accepting.

## The 6 agents in detail

### 1. Pitch Books

Builds decks for investors. Inputs: target company + sector + transaction type. Output: PowerPoint with:

- Cover + executive summary
- Investment thesis
- Market analysis with size and growth
- Comparables with a pre-formatted table (querying S&P / FactSet)
- Valuation (DCF, multiples, precedent transactions)
- Risks and mitigations

**When it shines**: when you've decided on the approach and need to materialize the deck. It doesn't replace strategic analysis.

### 2. Credit Memos

Traditional credit memo. Inputs: borrower + proposed structure + financial statements. Output:

- Description of the credit (amount, term, collateral)
- Financial analysis (ratios: DSCR, LTV, leverage, coverage)
- Borrower analysis (industry, management, track record)
- Collateral structure
- Risk flags (concentration, covenants, dependencies)
- Recommendation

**When it shines**: when the information is on the table and you need the formal document. It does **not** decide whether to approve or reject — that's on you.

### 3. KYC

Know Your Customer file:

- Legal identification (corporate structure, beneficial owners)
- Sanctions screening (querying LSEG / OFAC)
- Source of funds / source of wealth
- Risk rating per your institution's matrix
- Missing documentation listed

**When it shines**: banks and fintechs with regulated onboarding. For a small business that just needs to "check whether a client is legitimate," this is overkill.

### 4. Ledger Reconciliation

Reconciles accounts:

- Bank reconciliation (bank statement vs ledger)
- Intercompany reconciliation
- Accounts receivable / payable against subledger
- Identification of unreconciled items with a proposed adjustment

**When it shines**: month/quarter-end close. Cuts to hours what is otherwise days of manual work.

### 5. Actuarial Review

Review of actuarial models (insurance, pensions). Useful for verifying the consistency of a model built by someone else, not for building from scratch.

### 6. Monthly Close

The full monthly close:

- Adjustments and reclassifications
- Automatic accruals based on history
- P&L vs budget with variance analysis
- Reports for management with commentary

**When it shines**: companies with a ritualized monthly close where 70% is repetitive every month. The remaining 30% (things unique to that month) you flag yourself.

## How to turn it on

4 steps:

<Step number={1} title="Check your plan">

To use Excel + the Office add-in you need **Claude Pro or Max** (the $20/month plan doesn't include the Excel add-in on lower tiers). Check your account before you start.

</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Enable the plug-in in Cowork">

In Cowork → Settings → Plug-ins → **Financial Services** → Enable.

If it doesn't show up, your account may not have access yet (the rollout was gradual by region).

</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Connect the sources you're going to use">

You don't have to connect everything. Only the ones you'll use:

- Bloomberg / S&P / FactSet → only if you have them under contract
- Microsoft 365 → almost always needed
- PitchBook → if you work with private equity / VC

Each connector is OAuth with your account. The credentials stay with your provider, not with Claude.

</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Try it with a real file" last>

Open an Excel with real data (not a demo). Turn on Claude. Ask it for something concrete:

```
I have the general ledger for October 2025 in this Excel. Do the
reconciliation against the bank statement in the "Banco" tab.

Rules:
- Highlight in yellow the differences you can't explain
- For each difference, propose an adjustment with justification
- DO NOT modify the original numbers — add a "suggested adjustment" column
- Give me a summary in the first free cell
```

Claude does the work. You review before accepting changes.

</Step>

## 2 starter prompts

### Prompt 1 — Comparables for a valuation

```
I'm valuing [company] in the [sector] sector. I need a comparables table
for my model.

INPUTS:
- Target company: [name, ticker if public]
- Metrics to compare: EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E LTM and forward
- Universe: 8-12 public comparables + 5 recent transactions

OUTPUT in Excel:
- "Públicos" tab: table with the 8-12 companies + metrics + means and medians
- "Precedent Transactions" tab: table with the 5 transactions + multiples paid
- "Resumen" tab: implied valuation range for my target

Use S&P Capital IQ and FactSet. If a metric isn't available, mark it N/A.
DO NOT make up numbers.
```

### Prompt 2 — Credit memo for a loan

```
I need the credit memo for a financing proposal.

BORROWER: [company]
PROPOSED STRUCTURE:
- Amount: USD X
- Term: Y years
- Rate: Z% (fixed/variable)
- Collateral: [type]
- Covenants: [if any]

ATTACHED DOCUMENTATION: last 3 years of financial statements in this Excel

MEMO STRUCTURE (standard):
1. Executive summary + recommendation
2. Borrower description (industry, management, ownership)
3. Financial analysis (DSCR, LTV, leverage ratios, trends)
4. Transaction analysis (use of funds, sources & uses)
5. Collateral and structure
6. Risks and mitigations (industry, financial, operational)
7. Proposed terms

RULES:
- Calculate the actual ratios from the financial statements (don't make them up)
- If critical info is missing, mark it as "[missing info]"
- Final recommendation based on evidence, not optimism
```

## When it's NOT worth it

### 1. A small business with basic accounting

If your monthly close is 3 hours by hand with no Bloomberg or S&P, the plug-in is overkill. The [9 prompts for Cowork](/boveda/nueve-prompts-cowork) will probably serve you better.

### 2. You don't have the paid sources

Bloomberg costs ~$2k/month/seat. S&P Capital IQ ~$15k/year. If you don't have them, the agents can work with public data but lose much of their value.

### 3. A regulated sector where AI isn't accepted

Some banks/insurers still can't use AI in formal regulatory documents for compliance reasons. Check before setting up the flow.

### 4. You're going to blindly trust the outputs

**This is the most important thing**: the agents generate **80% drafts**. The remaining 20% — including any sensitive data — you have to review yourself. If your plan is "Claude does it, I approve without reading," don't use this in finance.

---

## The trap of pre-built agents

These 6 are good starting points but they're **opinionated**. Their structure, their chosen ratios, their formats — it all reflects decisions made by Anthropic + examples from top clients.

If your institution has its own standards (a different memo format, custom ratios, its own risk scale), the default outputs won't match. **You have to adjust them** for your institution. There are 2 options:

1. **Custom prompts**: give the agent your specific rules on each invocation
2. **Your own Skills**: build a skill that extends the agent with your rules

Option 2 scales better for recurring use.
