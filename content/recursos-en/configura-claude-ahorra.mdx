## The problem

Your plan is a **bucket of water**. Every message is like pouring out a glass. The problem: most people **leave the tap open** without realizing it. The water drains away, and the bucket runs dry by midday.

These 5 settings are about **closing the tap**. You're not using less Claude — you're just no longer **wasting** what you already have.

**Total setup: 5 minutes. Performance: 2x.**

| # | Setting | What it's for |
|---|---------------|----------|
| 1 | **Projects** | Files uploaded once, not in every chat |
| 2 | **Profile** | Claude always knows you without you repeating yourself |
| 3 | **Haiku for the simple stuff** | Don't use a Ferrari to go to the store |
| 4 | **Split usage into 2 blocks** | Take advantage of the 5h window |

## Setting 1 — Claude Projects

### The mistake

Every new chat, you upload the same files: brand guide, product list, tone of voice, your company data. Claude has to **read them from scratch every time**.

It's like bringing your résumé to your boss every morning so they remember who you are.

### The solution

**Projects** are folders with persistent context:
- You upload files **once**
- You set project instructions
- Every chat inside has that context **without spending tokens** re-loading it

### Step-by-step setup

1. Open `claude.ai`
2. Sidebar → **"Projects"**
3. **"Create Project"** → name it (e.g., "My Company", "Marketing")
4. Upload your files: brand guide, data, PDFs, whatever you always use
5. Add **custom instructions** — this is a prompt included in every chat
6. Every chat **inside the project** already has context

### Result

The same files that used to drain your account **10 times a day** are now read **once**.

See [Cowork workflow](/boveda/claude-cowork-workflow) for a more powerful desktop version with scheduled tasks.

## Setting 2 — Your Profile (Memory)

### The mistake

You start every conversation with:
- "I'm a designer"
- "Casual tone"
- "My company is called..."
- "My audience is..."

That's **3-5 messages** you waste just introducing yourself. Claude **doesn't remember you** between chats.

### The solution

The **Profile** is global context that applies to **all conversations**:

### Step-by-step setup

1. `claude.ai` → click your photo (bottom left corner)
2. **"Settings"**
3. **"Profile"** section
4. Write who you are, your professional context, your preferences

### Profile example

```
I'm the founder of a digital marketing agency in LATAM.

Context:
- Services: SEO, content, paid media for B2B SaaS
- Team: 5 people
- Main clients: Series A startups in LATAM

Preferences:
- Always respond in Latin American Spanish
- Professional but approachable tone
- Bullets, not long paragraphs
- Include metrics when relevant
- No emojis in formal docs
```

Claude applies this to **ALL** conversations automatically.

### Bonus

It works inside Projects too. You can have:
- A global profile (your general identity)
- A per-project profile (specific context)

Both are applied together.

## Setting 3 — Haiku for the simple stuff

### The mistake

You use **Sonnet** (a powerful model) for EVERYTHING:
- Proofreading a text
- Translating a paragraph
- Organizing a list
- Rephrasing a sentence

It's like using a **Ferrari to go to the corner store**. It works, but you're burning premium fuel.

### The solution

Claude has different "brains":

| Model | What it's for | Cost |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Opus** | Complex reasoning, critical decisions | The most expensive |
| **Sonnet** | Standard tasks, mid-size code | Balanced |
| **Haiku** | Mechanical, simple tasks | **Almost nothing** |

### When to use each

**Use Haiku for**:
- Fixing spelling/grammar
- Translating texts
- Organizing ideas into lists
- Answering direct questions
- Rephrasing a paragraph
- Synthesizing structured info

**Use Sonnet for**:
- Building code
- Analyzing long documents
- Business strategy
- Long, complex content
- Solving technical problems

**Use Opus for**:
- Architectural decisions
- Very complex debugging
- Deep multi-step analysis

### How to switch

Model selector above the text field → pick one. See [switch models](/boveda/cambia-de-modelo) for more options.

## Setting 4 — Split your usage into 2 blocks

### The mistake

You sit down with Claude **in one long stretch**. In 2 hours it's gone. You've got 6 hours of work left without Claude.

You burned through the whole bucket in one sitting.

### The truth about the 5h window

Claude has a **rolling 5-hour window**. Whatever you used at 9am **no longer counts** by 2pm — it's freed up.

It's like a buffet that lets you go back for seconds every 5h. If you eat everything in the first round, you're left with nothing. If you eat a little now and some later, **you eat all day**.

### How to organize yourself

| Time | What to do |
|------|-----------|
| 9-11am | Block 1: Claude for your most important tasks |
| 11am-2pm | Work WITHOUT Claude (emails, meetings, calls) |
| 2-4pm | Block 2: your usage reset, Claude again |
| 4-6pm | Work WITHOUT Claude |

If you organize your day into 2 blocks, you **double your plan** without paying more.

### Extra tip

During **off-peak hours**, the models run faster. See [five Claude mistakes](/boveda/cinco-errores-claude) — mistake #5 about peak hours.

## Setting 5 — Extra Usage (with a limit)

### The mistake

You're in the middle of an important project (a client presentation, code that almost works, a campaign wrapping up) and Claude **stops**.

You're left halfway, you lose momentum, and everything you'd built up goes cold.

### The solution

**Extra Usage** is a backup battery:
- When your normal plan runs out
- It kicks in automatically
- You're only charged for the extra you use
- **You set the monthly limit**

### Step-by-step setup

1. `claude.ai` → profile → **"Settings"**
2. **"Usage"** section
3. Enable **"Extra Usage"**
4. Set a **monthly limit** (start low: $5-10 USD)

### When to use it

✅ A critical project you can't stop
✅ A client waiting on a delivery
✅ A task with a hard deadline

❌ NOT for daily use
❌ NOT to replace a higher plan

It's a **safety net for critical moments**, not for constant use. If you use it regularly, better to **upgrade your plan** ([which Claude plan](/boveda/cual-plan-claude)).

## Anti-patterns

### 1. Applying everything on day 1

If you enable all 5 at once without understanding each one, you'll be confused. Better: 1 per day. **By day 5** you've got everything.

### 2. Using Haiku for everything

Haiku is **mechanical**, not deep reasoning. If you ask for complex strategy from Haiku, it'll give you something simplistic. **Each model for its task**.

### 3. Relying on "Extra Usage" as a plan

If you enable $50/month of extra and always spend it, **you should switch plans**. Max at $100 is cheaper than Pro at $20 + $50 extra.

### 4. Not reviewing your Profile periodically

Your role/company/context changes. **Every 3-6 months, review your Profile** and update it.

### 5. Projects with EVERYTHING mixed together

If you dump client A + client B + personal stuff into a single Project, **the context gets contaminated**. One project per separate context.

## When NOT to save (seriously)

If your critical work depends on Claude and you're on the Pro $20 plan, **don't obsess over saving**. The time you spend optimizing to save $20 would be better invested in the Max $100 plan and focusing on the work.

The 5 settings always apply. "Obsessing over saving" does not.
