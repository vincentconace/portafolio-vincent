## What it is

[browser-use](https://github.com/browser-use/browser-use) is one of the most popular frameworks for letting LLMs control browsers. Its differentiator: instead of handing the model raw HTML (expensive in tokens and confusing), it processes the DOM and passes a simplified, actionable representation. The model decides the action (click, type, scroll), browser-use executes it, and comes back.

This MCP packages browser-use as an MCP server. When Claude activates it, you get real browser capabilities: navigate, fill forms, scrape, log in with your session, test flows.

With 1,636 installs it's the second most adopted browser_automation MCP, after Chrome DevTools. That signals maturity.

```bash
npx claude-code-templates@latest --mcp browser_automation/browser-use-mcp-server
```

Prerequisites: Python + Chromium or Chrome installed. browser-use pulls its own deps.

For Claude:

```
Install the MCP browser_automation/browser-use-mcp-server. Verify that
I have Chrome installed and open a test session: navigate to
example.com, take a screenshot, and return the page title.
```

## What you can do with this

### Web task automation

"Go to Amazon, search for `noise cancelling headphones`, sort by reviews, and bring me the top 5 with price, rating, and review count." browser-use orchestrates the flow step by step.

### Assisted E2E testing

"Go to `localhost:3000/signup`, try the form with an invalid email + short password, verify it shows the correct errors, and screenshot it." More conversational than Cypress.

### Scraping with your own session

Unlike [BrightData](/boveda/mcp-brightdata) or [Apify](/boveda/mcp-apify), here you use YOUR session: your LinkedIn, your Twitter, your dashboard for any SaaS. Useful for tasks that require login.

### Onboarding to complex sites

"Go to the admin panel of [SaaS], navigate to `Settings > Integrations > API Keys` and show me which keys have been created." Without having to remember the path yourself.

### Compared to [Playwright Skill](/boveda/plugin-playwright-skill)

Playwright Skill is model-invoked and writes custom Playwright scripts for each task. Browser-use is more interactive: the agent navigates step by step, deciding what to do.

Key difference: browser-use is more conversational and robust to sites that change. Playwright Skill is better when you want a reproducible script.

## Prerequisites

- **Chrome / Chromium** installed
- **Python 3.x** (browser-use is Python)
- **Reasonable memory** (Chrome eats RAM)

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **Sites with strict TOS:** scraping LinkedIn, Instagram, etc. with your session can lead to an account ban. Use a dedicated account or read their TOS.
- **Token cost:** every action asks the model to decide what to do. Long workflows burn tokens.
- **For stable production automations:** traditional Playwright (no LLM) is more reliable. browser-use shines at exploration and one-off tasks.
- **Conflicts with your everyday Chrome:** running browser-use over your main profile can get weird (cookies, tabs). Use a dedicated profile or headless.
- **Latency:** every action is model + execution. Slower than a direct script.

</Callout>
