## What it is

A setting that configures `permissions.deny` with a list of paths/patterns that Claude can't touch:
- `.env*`
- `**/secrets/**`
- `**/*.pem`
- ...and more (4 patterns by default)

Difference from the [Env File Protection Hook](/boveda/hook-env-file-protection): the hook is trigger-based, pre-tool. This one is a permanent setting in the permission model. Defense in depth.

## What you can do with it

### Permanent permission model

At the settings level, Claude can't operate on denied paths. Stronger than hooks (which you can temporarily disable).

### Coverage out of the box

The 4 patterns cover the typical cases (.env, secrets, keys, certs). No custom setup needed.

### Combo with complementary hooks

- [Env File Protection Hook](/boveda/hook-env-file-protection) — pre-Write trigger
- [File Protection Hook](/boveda/hook-file-protection) — broader configurable paths
- **Deny Sensitive Files (this one)** — permanent baseline

### For autonomous sessions

Long `/goal` or `/loop` runs: the setting is a safety net you can't accidentally disable.

## When it's NOT a good fit

<Callout type="warning" title="Before installing">

- **If your legitimate workflow accesses .env:** the patterns will block you. Review the list.
- **If you need more granular control:** [File Protection Hook](/boveda/hook-file-protection) with custom configuration.
- **For repos without secrets:** less critical (but it won't hurt anyway).

</Callout>
