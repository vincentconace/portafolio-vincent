## What changed with Andromeda

Andromeda is the recommendation engine that Meta started using in earnest to decide who each ad gets shown to.

**Before**: you could compensate for a weak creative with a good interest stack.

**Now**: the **creative is the targeting**. What you say, how you say it, what shows up in the first frame and what your landing page says — that's what defines who Meta goes out to find.

Accounts with a system are scaling like never before. The ones without run in circles: they launch 40 ads with no method, one eats the budget, the rest get switched off, and nobody knows what made the winner win.

---

## The 3 conceptual pillars

### Pillar 1 — The creative IS the targeting

What you say in the ad (who you're talking to, what pain you name, what words you use) tells Meta who to show the ad to. **No interest stack fixes a generic ad**.

### Pillar 2 — Your landing page is targeting too

Andromeda **scrapes your landing page**, sees what people click, what they fill out. If the ad says one thing and the landing page another, the algorithm receives a **mixed signal** and distribution drops.

### Pillar 3 — The first frame is targeting

Second 0 of the video does targeting **before the first word**. A bedroom at night triggers one profile. A kitchen triggers another. A clinical white background triggers no one.

## The 8 rules

| # | Rule | In 1 line |
|---|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Volume is not strategy | Guessing with a bigger budget is not testing |
| 2 | Let go of audience targeting | Meta places better than you do |
| 3 | Creative + landing are the targeting | Andromeda reads both |
| 4 | Mine the customer's language | The winning hook doesn't come out of a meeting |
| 5 | Map every ad to awareness | Frequency 5-6 = you've run out of people at that level |
| 6 | Test angles across formats | One angle in a single format tells you nothing |

| 8 | Decision framework BEFORE launching | If you decide with a red dashboard, you decide with emotion |

## Rule 4 — Mine the customer's language

The winning hook doesn't come out of a meeting. It comes from:
- A frustrated customer posting on Reddit
- A review on Trustpilot
- A comment a competitor never answered

The work is to **go to those places and bring back the exact words** — without polishing them, without "improving" them.

### 4 sources and their prompts

#### Source 1 — Your own reviews

500 minimum. Load them into Claude and ask it to extract exact language:

```
You're a creative strategist for Meta Ads in 2026 (post-Andromeda).

I'm going to paste in my customers' reviews (500 minimum). Your job:

1. Extract the EXACT LANGUAGE they use to describe:
   - The pain before buying (literal pain point)
   - The solutions they tried and that failed
   - The desired outcome (how they imagined their life after)
   - The "aha moment" (what finally made them buy)

2. Give me back 15 distinct hook angles based on VERBATIM
   phrases. DON'T paraphrase: copy the customer's words.

3. For each hook, assign an awareness level (Unaware /
   Problem aware / Solution aware /
   Product aware / Ready to buy).

4. Star the 3 angles most likely to
   scale beyond the current bottom-of-funnel.

ultrathink — I want in-depth analysis, not a summary.

Reviews:
[PASTE YOUR REVIEWS HERE]
```

#### Source 2 — Reddit

You're looking for the pain (NOT your brand). Sort by upvotes.

```
You're a market researcher for Meta Ads. I'm going to paste posts
and comments from Reddit about [TOPIC / CUSTOMER PAIN].

Your job:
1. Extract the 10 most up-voted phrases that describe the pain
   in the customer's literal language.
2. Identify the 5 "common enemies" they mention (products
   they tried and that failed, brands that let them down, myths
   they repeat).
3. Give me back 10 hooks ready for short video (UGC / podcast
   style) using those verbatim phrases.
4. For each hook, suggest the first frame (setting, time,
   camera) — remember the first frame is targeting.

ultrathink — emotional and identity patterns, not just
descriptive ones.

Posts:
[PASTE HERE]
```

#### Source 3 — Comments on competitor ads

Where people say **exactly what stopped them from buying**.

```
You're a creative auditor. I'm going to paste the comments from
my competitors' best-performing ads on Meta.

Your job:
1. Identify the 5 most repeated OBJECTIONS (what stopped
   people from buying from them).
2. Identify the 5 most repeated PRAISES (what they did like
   but wasn't enough to convert).
3. For each objection, write me a hook that dissolves it in
   the first 3 seconds.
4. For each praise my competitors aren't taking advantage of,
   suggest an angle of attack for me.

Be brutally honest about where I'm losing.

Comments:
[PASTE HERE]
```

#### Source 4 — Your own account (90 days)

The data you already have.

```
You're a growth marketer doing a post-Andromeda audit. I'm
sending you the report from the last 90 days of my Meta account
(spend, CPA, ROAS, frequency, hook rate, hold rate per ad).

Your job:
1. Identify the 3 ads that spent the most and dissect WHAT
   made them work (hook, angle, format, first frame,
   awareness level they were targeting).
2. Detect whether there's a pattern in what scales vs what dies.
3. Map EVERY active ad to an awareness level. Tell me
   what % of spend is concentrated in each level.
4. Tell me what the GAP is: which awareness level I'm NOT
   targeting and why that's capping my spend ceiling.
5. Propose 5 new angles to cover the gap.

ultrathink — the spend ceiling isn't broken with more
bottom-of-funnel.

Report:
[PASTE HERE]
```

## Rule 5 — Map every ad to awareness

80-90% of the creative in most accounts is **bottom-of-funnel**. When frequency hits 5-6, it's not that you've run out of creativity — **you've run out of people at that level**.

### The 5 levels

| Level | State | Hook example |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 — Unaware | Doesn't know they have a problem | "If your dog already knows bath time is coming before you even turn on the tap, there's something you're not seeing." |
| 2 — Problem aware | Recognizes the pain, isn't looking for a solution | "Does it frustrate you that every time you search for something in your inbox it takes 10 minutes?" |
| 3 — Solution aware | Tried others and failed | "You tried 3 CRMs and they all ended up as an abandoned spreadsheet. See why." |
| 4 — Product aware | Knows your brand, hasn't bought | "You know we exist. Here are the 3 reasons people didn't regret it." |
| 5 — Ready to buy | Just needs the offer | "Today only: annual plan with 2 months free." |

### Prompt — awareness classifier

```
I'm going to paste my list of active ads (copy + description
of the visual). For each one:

1. Assign an awareness level.
2. Justify briefly (what specific phrase tells you).
3. At the end, give me the % of my ads at each level.
4. If more than 80% is in "Product aware" or "Ready to
   buy", propose 5 hooks for "Unaware" and
   "Problem aware".

ultrathink — frequency 5-6 doesn't mean you've run out of
creativity, it means you've run out of people at that level.

Ads:
[PASTE HERE]
```

## Rules 1-2-3 — Creative + landing + first frame

3 rules that are really one: **everything the algorithm sees counts as targeting**.

### How to brief the first frame

When you hand the brief to the creator, don't just tell them the hook. Tell them the **exact setting, time of day, wardrobe, camera, what's visible in the background**. So specific they can't improvise.

```
I'm going to brief a creator for an ad. I need a brief with an
EXPLICIT first frame.

Context:
- Product: [PASTE]
- Audience (customer language): [PASTE]
- Awareness level: [Unaware / Problem aware / etc.]
- Angle: [PASTE]
- Hook: [PASTE]

Generate a brief that includes:
1. First frame (second 0): setting, time of day, wardrobe,
   camera, what's visible in the background. So specific the creator
   can't improvise.
2. Seconds 1-3: how the hook connects with the visual.
3. Seconds 3-15: development of the angle (without drifting).
4. Seconds 15-30: proof / social proof / demo.
5. CTA + landing: copy for the final frame and suggested link. The
   landing page has to repeat the same language as the hook.

Be brutally specific. A vague brief = a generic ad.
```

### Ad ↔ landing coherence audit

```
Andromeda reads my landing page and my ads. If they say different
things, I lose distribution.

I'm sending you:
- Ad (copy + visual description): [PASTE]
- Landing page (URL or text): [PASTE]

Audit:
1. Does the ad's hook appear VERBATIM in the first scroll
   of the landing page? If not, why not?
2. Is the ad's angle the dominant angle of the landing page,
   or is the landing page selling a different pitch?
3. Does the ad's awareness level match the landing page?
   (Unaware → educational landing page, not a direct checkout.)
4. Are the CTAs coherent? Are they measuring the same event?
5. List of discrepancies + the exact fix for each one.

ultrathink — the algorithm is reading mixed signals. That
explains why a good ad sometimes doesn't scale.
```

## Rules 6-7 — Testing and winners

### The important difference

- **Angle**: the idea the ad is built on. The frustration or desire that makes the right person feel the ad was made for them.
- **Hook**: the first 3 seconds. The way you open the angle.
- **Format**: the vehicle (UGC, static, podcast, street).

Same angle in UGC + static + podcast → if all 3 die, the angle is the problem. If one scales, the format was the variable.

### Prompt — 5 hook variations

```
I have a validated angle that works. I want 5 hook variations
(first 3 sec) to test the same concept.

Validated angle:
[DESCRIBE IT AND WHY IT WORKS]

Your job:
1. Generate 5 hooks that attack the SAME angle from 5
   different emotional entry points:
   - Hook 1: direct question about the pain
   - Hook 2: visual pattern interrupt + striking phrase
   - Hook 3: contrast (before/after or what they think vs reality)
   - Hook 4: confessional testimonial
   - Hook 5: statistic or specific data point
2. For each hook, describe the first frame (setting, wardrobe,
   camera).
3. Suggest the ideal format for each (UGC, static, podcast, street).

ultrathink — I want REAL variation, not the same words
reordered.
```

### Prompt — extend a winner

```
I have a winning ad. I want to squeeze the juice out of it before it fatigues.

Winning ad:
- Hook: [PASTE]
- Angle: [PASTE]
- Format: [UGC / static / podcast / street]
- Metrics: [CPA, ROAS, spend, frequency]

Give me 3 extension strategies:
1. Frankenstein: combine this hook with the body of ANOTHER ad
   that already worked.
2. Same angle, different format: turn this UGC into static,
   podcast and street interview.
3. 5 hook variations: 5 new ways to open the same
   angle.

ultrathink — the winner is a signal, not a destination.
```

## Rule 8 — Decision framework BEFORE launching

Two mistakes that kill accounts:
- **Switching off too soon**: if it's spending, the algorithm is learning. Give it 3-5 days minimum
- **Holding on out of hope**: if after several weeks and significant spend it doesn't hit the target, holding on is burning budget

### 3 principles

1. **Trust spend over per-ad ROAS**. Meta optimizes the whole account, not ad by ad. An ad with low ROAS that spends a lot is often doing invisible work.

2. **Optimize for PURCHASE**. Always. Train wrong → find wrong. Accounts that optimize for ATC, view content or traffic find the wrong people with precision.

3. **Write your criteria BEFORE launching**. If you define them with a red dashboard, you decide with emotion.

### Prompt — decision framework

```
I want to define my decision framework BEFORE launching the next
batch — so I DON'T decide under pressure while it's spending.

Data:
- Target CPA: [X]
- Target ROAS: [X]
- Average daily spend: [X]
- Category / margin: [PASTE]

Generate a framework with:
1. Review threshold: at what spend do I review an ad?
   (3-5 days minimum if it's spending)
2. Kill threshold: what CPA/ROAS at how much spend = kill?
3. Soft metrics that signal potential: hook rate, hold rate,
   CTR, thumb-stop. Thresholds that say "it has juice even if
   CPA is high"
4. Top-spender rule: if an ad spends a lot even though
   per-ad ROAS is low, when do I NOT switch it off?
5. Pixel optimization: confirm I'm optimizing for
   PURCHASE.

Return it as a printable decision-tree chart to stick next
to Ads Manager.

ultrathink — I don't want generic, I want a framework for MY margin
and spend velocity.
```

## Why the 8 matter together

Andromeda **learns from consistency**. If every ad tells a different story, the algorithm resets. If you feed it a system — same language, same angle across formats, same awareness level attacked in several ways — **it compounds**.

That's why these 8 work together: no single one is enough on its own.

## What most people are still getting wrong in 2026

- **Optimizing for ATC, view content or traffic** instead of purchase
- **Switching off the top-spender** because its per-ad ROAS is low
- **Throwing out 40 ads with no system** and calling it "testing"
